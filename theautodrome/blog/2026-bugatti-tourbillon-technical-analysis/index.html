Bugatti Tourbillon: An Analog Masterpiece in a Digital Age

In an era where automotive excellence is increasingly measured by lines of code, Molsheim has delivered a profound counter-argument. The Bugatti Tourbillon is not merely a successor to the Chiron; it is a deliberate pivot toward the eternal.

At its heart lies a naturally aspirated 8.3-liter V16 engine, developed with Cosworth. This 1,000-horsepower marvel, augmented by three electric motors, brings output to a staggering 1,800 horsepower. The interior is a testament to this "analog-first" philosophy, featuring a complex, skeletonized instrument cluster made of titanium, sapphires, and rubies.

At The Autodrome, we view the Tourbillon as the gold standard for high-fidelity vehicles: a perfect balance of heritage, technical mastery, and uncompromising performance.
