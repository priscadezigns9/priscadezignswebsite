Ghost Squadron GS26: The Mallorca Convoy

The return of the Ghost Squadron is never a quiet affair. For GS26, the Koenigsegg family descended upon the crystalline shores and winding mountain passes of Mallorca, Spain. This wasn't just a tour; it was a high-fidelity demonstration of automotive mastery, where the world's most advanced megacars reclaimed the asphalt of the Mediterranean.

The Convoy: Engineering in Motion

The scenic convoy, featuring a multi-million dollar lineup including the Jesko, CC850, and Regera, carved through the island's most challenging routes. From the technical hairpins leading to Es Guix to the sweeping coastal vistas of Port de Sóller, the Ghost Squadron displayed a level of mechanical poise that defies conventional physics.

The sound of twin-turbo V8s and the revolutionary Light Speed Transmission (LST) echoing through the Serra de Tramuntana mountains provided a symphony of performance. Each vehicle in the squadron represents the pinnacle of power-to-weight ratio and aerodynamic efficiency, serving as a rolling testament to Christian von Koenigsegg's uncompromising vision.

Island Mastery: Routes of the Elite

The three-day intensive driving event pushed both man and machine. Routes included the iconic descent into Sa Calobra and the long, high-speed stretches toward the Cap de Formentor lighthouse. These roads, known for their demanding geometry, were the perfect canvas for the Koenigsegg active chassis systems to showcase their adaptability.

At Es Guix, the squadron took a tactical pause, allowing enthusiasts and collectors to appreciate the artisanal craftsmanship of the interiors—a blend of carbon fiber, polished aluminum, and the finest leathers, all meticulously assembled in Ängelholm.
