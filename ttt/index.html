Common Concerns

Myths vs. Facts

"Chinese EVs are unreliable."
BYD is the world's largest EV manufacturer, surpassing Tesla in 2023. GAC has delivered over 1.5 million EVs. JD Power and Euro NCAP consistently rate BYD and GAC at or above Japanese and Korean competitors. Denza is a joint venture with Mercedes-Benz.
"What if the battery fails?"
Every Evolve vehicle carries an 8-year manufacturer warranty on the battery. Degradation averages just 2.3% per year — after 8 years the battery still holds over 81% of original capacity.
"EVs don't have enough range."
The GAC Aion V reaches 600km on a full charge. Trinidad & Tobago is approximately 80km end to end — a single charge covers an entire week of typical corporate driving without a top-up.
"EVs are more likely to catch fire."
The opposite is true. Electric vehicles experience 25 fires per 100,000 vehicles. Petrol vehicles: 1,530 per 100,000 — over 60× higher. No fuel tank, no combustible liquid, no hot exhaust.
"Trinidad's roads aren't made for EVs."
Every Evolve vehicle is tested and delivered for Caribbean road conditions — potholes, heat, humidity, and all. The suspension systems on the Aion V, C10, and Sealion 7 are built for mixed terrain. Trinidad is also approximately 80km end to end — well within a single charge. These vehicles were engineered for real-world use, not just smooth highways.
