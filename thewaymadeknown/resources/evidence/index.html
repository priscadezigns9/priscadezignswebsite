The Cohesion of Truth

Christianity is not a blind leap into the dark. It is an informed decision based on historical documentation, eyewitness testimony, and the analytical pursuit of the Truth.

Historical

The Bible is a collection of biographies, letters, poetry, and songs documented by real people in real time. We treat the Gospels as a "Cold Case"—analyzing them with the same rigor used in forensic investigations.

Analytical

We welcome open-mindedness. Informed decisions require analyzing various belief systems to see which one truly coheres. Faith is the result of evidence meeting a seeking heart.

Documentary

Unlike imaginary stories, the Christian faith is rooted in specific human history and geographical reality. Documentation is the bridge from the ancient world to our modern understanding.

Recommended Reading

Cold-Case Christianity by J. Warner Wallace. A homicide detective investigates the claims of the Gospels, treating them as eyewitness accounts.
The Three Tests for Truth Understanding logical consistency, empirical adequacy, and experiential relevance in the pursuit of a coherent worldview.

Analytical Faith

We thank God for the analytical mind—the part of us that questions, researches, and refuses to accept stories at face value. In the pursuit of Truth, every stone should be turned.
