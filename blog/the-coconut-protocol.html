The Coconut Protocol: Roadside Artisanal Extraction.

In the Caribbean, the coconut is more than just an ingredient; it is a biological conduit. For generations, the true power of our recipes—the velvety finish of a Callaloo or the rich depth of an Oil Down—has depended on the quality of the coconut milk. But not all milk is created equal.

The Artisanal Hub

We track our sourcing back to what we call "Artisanal Hubs." These are the roadside vendors and regional estates who still perform artisanal extraction. Unlike industrial, canned alternatives, fresh-pressed coconut milk retains its active fats and micronutrient density.

The "Protocol" is simple but strict: the coconut must be harvested at peak maturity, grated within hours, and pressed with a precise amount of warm water to extract the first-press cream. This is the Alpha Fat that defines our heritage provisions.

Why Authenticity Matters

When you use industrial substitutes, you lose the subtle sweetness and the unique mouthfeel that fresh coconut oil provides. By documenting these sourcing nodes and extraction methods, Calalloo ensures that the chemical integrity of our heritage food remains intact for the next generation.
