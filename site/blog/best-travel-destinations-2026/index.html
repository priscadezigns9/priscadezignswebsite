Top Travel Destinations You Need to Visit in 2026

The world is calling, and 2026 is shaping up to be one of the best years in recent memory to answer. From bucket-list islands to emerging destinations that savvy travellers are discovering before the crowds arrive, here are the top travel destinations you need to add to your itinerary this year.

The Maldives - Still Unmissable

Overwater bungalows, turquoise lagoons, and some of the world's finest marine life - the Maldives remains the gold standard of luxury travel. In 2026, new eco-focused resorts are offering solar-powered villas and coral regeneration programmes that let you contribute to preservation of these fragile atolls while you holiday. The best time to visit is November through April, and booking six months in advance is strongly recommended for top properties.

Albania - Europe's Hidden Gem

If you want Mediterranean beauty without Mediterranean crowds and prices, Albania in 2026 is your answer. The Albanian Riviera stretches from Sarande to Himara with jaw-dropping beaches, crystal-clear water, and charming coastal towns largely undiscovered by mass tourism. Inland, the ancient city of Berat offers Byzantine churches, Ottoman-era architecture, and traditional raki in atmospheric hilltop bars. Budget travellers and luxury seekers alike will find Albania delivers extraordinary value.

Japan - The Return of Refined Travel

Japan has refined its approach to tourism management in 2026, introducing visitor limits to key sites in Kyoto and Nara that have actually improved the experience dramatically. The spring cherry blossom season and the autumn maple season remain transformative experiences. Beyond the classics, the Tohoku region and Kyushu continue to offer bullet train access, world-class onsen culture, and Michelin-starred dining at a fraction of Tokyo prices.

Colombia - South America's Rising Star

Cartagena's walled city has always been magnificent, but 2026 sees Colombia establishing itself as a full-spectrum destination. The Coffee Region, the lost city trek in the Sierra Nevada, and the emerging culinary scene in Medellin are drawing sophisticated travellers who want culture, adventure, and world-class food. Colombian airlines have also dramatically expanded international routes, making access easier than ever.

Rwanda - Sustainable Travel Done Right

Rwanda in 2026 is the definitive model of sustainable tourism. Gorilla trekking permits in Volcanoes National Park fund conservation and community development programmes directly. The country's extraordinary commitment to environmental protection creates a travel experience that feels genuinely responsible. The expanded offerings in Nyungwe Forest make Rwanda a compelling multi-week destination.

Saudi Arabia - The New Frontier

Saudi Arabia's tourism opening continues to deliver surprises. AlUla's dramatic sandstone formations and heritage sites rival Petra in scale and atmosphere. The Red Sea coastline is developing world-class dive sites with almost entirely untouched reefs. Luxury hotels, excellent local food, and warm hospitality make Saudi Arabia one of 2026's most surprising travel stories.

Portugal - Still the Smart Choice

Lisbon and Porto remain two of Europe's most walkable and culturally rich cities. The Alentejo and Algarve regions offer space and quiet alongside wine, ceramics, and phenomenal seafood. The NHT programme makes it easier than ever to stay in converted castles, palaces, and monasteries at surprisingly accessible prices.

Travel in 2026 is about intentionality - choosing destinations that reward curiosity, support local communities, and create memories that last a lifetime. The world rewards the well-prepared traveller.
