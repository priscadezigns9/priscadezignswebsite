From Raw Data to Real Decisions: A Practical Guide

May 2026 · 8 min read

Most organisations are better at collecting data than using it. They build pipelines, populate dashboards, run reports and then make the same decisions they would have made without any of it. The problem is not a lack of data. It is a missing process for turning data into action.

Start with the decision, not the data

The most common analytics mistake is starting with data and hoping insight emerges. It rarely does. Start with the decision you need to make. What is the choice in front of you? What would it take to make it confidently? Working backward from decision to data is more efficient and more rigorous. It forces you to ask: what specific information would change my mind? If no data could change your mind, the analysis is theatre.

Define the minimum viable insight

You do not need perfect data. You need data good enough to act on given the cost of waiting versus the cost of being wrong. In most business decisions, a directionally correct answer from imperfect data is more valuable than a precise answer that arrives too late. Define upfront what level of confidence is enough and what error rate is acceptable given the stakes.

Distinguish correlation from causation

Your data will show you patterns. Most of them are correlations, things that travel together without one causing the other. Acting on a correlation as if it were a cause is one of the most expensive mistakes in business analytics. Before building strategy around a correlation, ask: is there a plausible mechanism? Can we test it with a controlled experiment? What would disprove this?

Build a decision log

Document every significant decision: what data you used, what assumptions you made, what you decided, and what outcome you expected. Review these logs quarterly. This creates a feedback loop between decisions and results, the only way analytical skill actually compounds.

Ship and measure

Every decision should come with a measurement plan: what metric will tell us whether this worked, over what time window, at what threshold? Decisions without measurement plans produce learning at half the rate of decisions with them. Data to decision to action to measurement to better data. Each pass around this loop makes the organisation smarter.
