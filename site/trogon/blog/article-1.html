The 5 Metrics Every Growing Business Must Track

May 2026 · 7 min read

Most businesses drown in data while starving for insight. They track dozens of metrics, build elaborate dashboards, and still make decisions based on gut feeling because nothing they are measuring tells them what they need to know. The fix is not more data. It is the right data.

1. Customer Acquisition Cost

How much does it cost to acquire one paying customer? Divide your total sales and marketing spend by the number of new customers acquired in the same period. If you do not know your CAC, you cannot know whether your growth is profitable. A business growing at 50% per year with a CAC higher than customer lifetime value is not growing. It is consuming capital to delay collapse.

2. Customer Lifetime Value

LTV is what a customer is worth to your business over the entire relationship. The LTV to CAC ratio is arguably the most important number in any subscription or repeat-purchase business. A healthy ratio is 3:1 or higher. Improving LTV does not always mean raising prices. Reducing churn, increasing purchase frequency, and expanding what customers buy from you all compound into higher LTV over time.

3. Churn Rate

Churn is the silent killer. A 5% monthly churn rate sounds manageable until you realise it means replacing your entire customer base every 20 months just to stay flat. Track churn by cohort to see whether newer customers retain better or worse than older ones.

4. Revenue per Employee

This is a proxy for operational efficiency. As you hire, does revenue grow faster than headcount? If revenue per employee is declining as you scale, you are building complexity faster than capacity. Strong companies in most industries target between $200,000 and $500,000 in revenue per employee.

5. Net Promoter Score

NPS is a leading indicator. Customers who would recommend you are not just satisfied, they are advocates. A declining NPS is a warning that revenue problems are on their way. Track it by customer segment, not just overall. Your best and at-risk customers require completely different responses.
