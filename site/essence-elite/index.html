Frequently Asked Questions

How do I choose a signature scent?

Start with your instinct — notes that draw you in when you sample. Test on skin (not paper), wear for 2–4 hours, and evaluate dry-down. Your signature is the one you reach for without thinking.

What is the difference between EDP and EDT?

Eau de Parfum (EDP) has 15–20% fragrance concentration — lasts 6–8 hours. Eau de Toilette (EDT) has 5–15% — lasts 3–5 hours. EDP is richer and more projection-forward.

How long does a perfume last?

Applied to pulse points (wrists, neck, inner elbow), an EDP lasts 6–8 hours. Fragrance longevity is also affected by skin type — moisturized skin holds scent significantly longer.

What is a fragrance family?

Fragrance families classify scents by dominant character: Floral (rose, jasmine), Woody (sandalwood, cedar), Oriental (amber, oud, vanilla), Fresh (citrus, green, aquatic), and Fougère (lavender, oakmoss).

How should I store my perfume?

Away from direct sunlight, heat, and humidity. A cool, dark drawer or cabinet is ideal. Avoid the bathroom — temperature fluctuations degrade the composition. Keep in the original bottle.

What are the best-selling fragrances of 2026?

The market leaders remain: Maison Margiela Replica 'By the Fireplace', Dior Sauvage (men), YSL Black Opium (women), Lattafa Khamrah (value), and Le Labo Santal 33 (niche).
