CONCENTRATION GUIDE

The Difference Between
Eau de Parfum
and Everything Else.

Most people pick a fragrance by how it smells in the bottle. The professionals pick by concentration — because that determines how long it lasts, how it develops on skin, and how much of it you actually need.

01

Parfum (20–30%)

The purest form. One or two sprays in the morning and you're still leaving traces at midnight. Reserved for signature scents worth owning.

02

Eau de Parfum (15–20%)

The sweet spot. Four to six hours of real presence. This is what most of our curated edit features.

03

Eau de Toilette (5–15%)

Lighter, brighter. Perfect for warmer months when you want presence without weight. Reapply with intention.

04

Eau de Cologne (2–5%)

Fleeting by design. The morning spritz, the gym bag essential. Refreshing rather than anchoring.

THE ART OF WEARING SCENT

Five rules for a
scent that stays.

Most people have never been taught how to wear fragrance. Here's what the professionals know that most people don't.

1

Pulse points, not clothes.

Wrist, neck, inner elbow. Heat activates the oil and projects the scent. Spraying on fabric kills the drydown.

2

Don't rub.

Rubbing breaks the molecular structure of the top notes. Spray, then let it settle.

3

Layer from the shower.

Unscented body wash → matching body lotion → fragrance. The lotion gives the oil something to cling to. It lasts twice as long.

4

Let it breathe.

A fragrance has three acts — top notes (first 15 mins), heart (2 hours), base (all day). Don't judge a fragrance by the bottle or the immediate spray.

5

One occasion, one scent.

The brain links scent to memory faster than any other sense. Wear the same scent to every important meeting, trip, or moment. You're building an olfactory signature.
