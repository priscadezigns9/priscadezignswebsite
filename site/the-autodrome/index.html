Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I wash my car?

Every 2 weeks is the benchmark. Wash more frequently if driving in salt (coastal/winter), construction dust, or heavy rain. Never let bird droppings or tree sap sit — they etch paint within 48 hours.

What is paint correction and do I need it?

Paint correction removes swirl marks, light scratches, and oxidation using machine polishing. If your paint looks hazy or dull in direct sunlight, a single-stage correction ($200–600 at a detailer) transforms the finish.

What is the best way to protect car paint?

Ceramic coating (3–5 year protection, hardness 9H), followed by paint protection film (PPF) for high-impact areas. For budget protection, a quality carnauba wax or paint sealant every 3–4 months.

How do I remove scratches from my car?

Light surface scratches (nail test — if your nail doesn't catch, it's clearcoat only): use a dual-action polisher with a cutting compound. Deep scratches through primer need a professional touch-up.

When should I change my cabin air filter?

Every 15,000–25,000 miles or annually. Signs it's overdue: reduced A/C airflow, musty smell from vents, increased allergy symptoms inside the car. A $15 filter swap takes 10 minutes.

What car accessories actually improve driving?

Dashcam (legal protection, accident evidence), seat cushion for long drives, a quality phone mount, all-weather floor mats, and a portable jump starter. These five are the practical essentials every driver needs.
