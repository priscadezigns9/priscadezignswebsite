How to Choose the Right Dash Cam in 2026

Dash cameras have become essential kit for modern drivers. But with hundreds of options on the market, choosing the right one is tricky. Here's what actually matters.

Resolution: 1080p vs 4K

1080p is the minimum you should consider in 2026. It captures licence plates clearly in daylight. 4K is worth it if you drive at night frequently or in areas with complex traffic situations — the detail difference is significant when reviewing footage.

Field of View

A 140-160 degree wide-angle lens captures multiple lanes without distortion. Anything below 120 degrees misses too much. Avoid ultra-wide lenses above 170 degrees — they introduce excessive fisheye distortion at the edges.

Night Vision

Look for Sony STARVIS or similar low-light sensors. A dash cam that captures clear footage at night is far more valuable than one that looks great in daylight demos but struggles in real conditions.

Front and Rear vs Front Only

Rear-end collisions are among the most common. A dual-channel cam (front + rear) costs roughly 30-40% more than front-only but covers you for the claims that matter most.

Parking Mode

If you park on public roads or in multi-storey car parks, hardwired parking mode records impacts and motion while the car is off. Requires professional fitting but provides 24/7 protection.

Recommended Features Checklist

  • 4K front resolution
  • Rear camera included
  • Sony STARVIS night sensor
  • Loop recording with auto-overwrite
  • Parking mode (hardwire kit)
  • GPS logging (speed and location stamped)
