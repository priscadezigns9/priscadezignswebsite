Must-Have Car Accessories 2026: Upgrade Your Ride

The right accessories turn a good car into a great daily experience. These are the upgrades that actually make a difference — not gimmicks, not filler — ranked by how much they improve everyday driving.

Safety and Visibility

1. Dash cam (front + rear): 4K front cameras capture licence plates and accident details clearly. Models with cloud backup mean footage survives even if the camera is stolen. 2. Blind spot mirrors: convex stick-on mirrors for wider field of view around pillars. Simple, cheap, genuinely useful. 3. Tyre pressure monitoring system (TPMS): external sensors that show real-time PSI for each tyre on a dashboard display. Under-inflated tyres are the leading cause of blowouts.

Comfort and Convenience

1. Wireless CarPlay/Android Auto adapter: converts wired CarPlay to wireless for $80-$120 — no more reaching for the cable. 2. Seat cushion memory foam: transforms long drives if your car has poor stock seat cushioning. 3. Car humidifier/air purifier: particularly valuable for daily commuters — significantly reduces fatigue on long drives.

Tech Upgrades

1. OBD2 scanner + app (Bluetooth): plugs into your car's diagnostic port, gives live engine data and reads/clears fault codes. Essential for any car owner. $30 for the scanner. 2. Wireless charging pad for centre console: particularly valuable for 2018-2023 vehicles that didn't come with factory wireless charging. 3. Head-up display (HUD): projects speed, navigation, and incoming calls onto the windscreen. Keeps eyes on the road.

Detailing and Maintenance

1. Ceramic spray coating: $30 and 30 minutes twice a year protects paint better than any wax. 2. UV-protective dashboard cover: prevents cracking and fading of the dashboard — typically costs $30-$50 custom-fit. 3. Microfiber detailing kit: keep one in the boot for quick clean-ups. Stains removed immediately never become permanent stains.
