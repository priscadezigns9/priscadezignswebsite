From Wireframe to Launch: A Complete App Design Checklist

May 2026 · 6 min read

Most app failures are not failures of vision. They are failures of process. The idea was good. The execution missed something. A systematic checklist does not eliminate creativity. It protects it by ensuring the fundamentals are covered at every stage.

Discovery and definition

  • Define the one core problem the app solves
  • Identify the primary user and their context of use
  • Map the user journey from problem awareness to core value
  • Define success metrics before you build anything

Wireframing

  • Sketch key flows before committing to visual design
  • Validate the information architecture with real users
  • Test the empty state, error state, and loading state for every screen
  • Confirm all primary actions sit within thumb reach zones

Visual design

  • Establish a design system before designing individual screens
  • Check colour contrast ratios for accessibility compliance
  • Verify all touch targets meet the 44pt minimum
  • Test your designs on multiple device sizes and screen densities

Pre-launch

  • Conduct usability testing with users who have never seen the app
  • Verify onboarding delivers value before requesting account creation
  • Test every error state and edge case
  • Review app store listing: screenshots, description, and preview video

Post-launch

  • Monitor crash rates and ANR rates from day one
  • Read every early review and categorise the feedback
  • Track retention curves by cohort from the first week
  • Define the first post-launch iteration before you launch

Design your app with the right process from the start
