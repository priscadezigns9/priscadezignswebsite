The 5 Copywriting Formulas That Never Go Out of Style

May 2026 · 6 min read

Copywriting formulas exist because human psychology does not change. The same motivations, fears, and desires that drove people to act in 1950 drive them today. These five frameworks have proven their value across decades, mediums, and industries.

1. AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action

The oldest and most used formula in copywriting. Capture attention with a headline that stops the right person. Build interest with relevant, specific information. Create desire by connecting features to outcomes the reader wants. Drive action with a clear, low-friction next step. AIDA works because it mirrors the natural decision-making process.

2. PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution

State the problem the reader recognises. Deepen it by exploring the frustration, the cost, the consequences of leaving it unsolved. Then present your solution as the clear path out. PAS is particularly effective for products that solve real pain points, because it meets the reader exactly where they are.

3. BAB — Before, After, Bridge

Describe the reader's current reality. Paint the picture of a better future state. Then present your product or service as the bridge between the two. BAB is optimistic rather than pain-focused, which makes it more appropriate for aspirational products and services.

4. FAB — Features, Advantages, Benefits

This framework forces you to translate product features into things that matter to the reader. Feature: what the product does. Advantage: why that is better than alternatives. Benefit: what the customer actually gains. Most copy stops at features. The benefit is what sells.

5. The 4 Cs — Clear, Concise, Compelling, Credible

Not a sequential formula but a quality checklist. Is every sentence clear? Is it as short as it can be while retaining meaning? Does it give the reader a reason to keep reading? Is it believable and backed by evidence? Copy that fails any of these four tests should be revised before it runs.
