How to Find a Trusted Plumber (Without the Stress)

Plumbing problems don't wait for convenient times. Here's how to find a qualified professional quickly, safely, and without overpaying.

A burst pipe. A blocked drain at midnight. A water heater that finally gives up. Plumbing emergencies have a habit of arriving at the worst possible moments — and when they do, you need help fast. But rushing to hire the first person you find can be a costly mistake.

The good news? Finding a trustworthy plumber doesn't have to be a gamble. With the right approach, you can hire with confidence — even under pressure.

1. Start With Referrals, Then Verify

Word of mouth is still one of the most reliable ways to find a good plumber. Ask neighbours, friends, or family who they've used and whether they'd hire them again. But don't stop there — a good referral is just a starting point. You should still verify that the person is qualified and insured before letting them into your home.

2. Check Licensing and Insurance

Any reputable plumber should be able to show proof of licensing and liability insurance. Licensing means they've met professional standards in their trade. Insurance protects you if something goes wrong during the job — a burst pipe during a repair, for example, or accidental property damage. If a plumber can't provide either, walk away.

"A licensed, insured plumber isn't just better for the job — they're better for your peace of mind. Don't skip this step, even in an emergency."

3. Ask the Right Questions Before You Hire

Before committing to anyone, ask:

  • Do you provide a written quote before starting? Verbal estimates can balloon once work begins.
  • Is there a call-out fee? Some plumbers charge just to show up — know this upfront.
  • What's your warranty on labour and parts? A professional stands behind their work.
  • Have you handled this type of job before? Plumbing has specialists — not every plumber handles gas lines, solar water heaters, or drainage systems equally well.
  • Can you provide references? Even a couple of past clients' names speaks volumes.

4. Watch for Red Flags

Not every plumber operates with your best interest in mind. Be cautious of anyone who:

  • Refuses to give a written estimate
  • Quotes a suspiciously low price, then adds charges later
  • Pressures you into same-day decisions on major work
  • Has no online presence, reviews, or references
  • Asks for full payment upfront before doing any work

5. Check Reviews — But Read Them Critically

Online reviews can be helpful, but look for patterns rather than outliers. A single bad review doesn't necessarily mean a bad plumber — but consistent complaints about delays, overcharging, or shoddy work should be taken seriously. Also look at how the plumber responds to negative feedback. Professionalism in replies often reflects professionalism on the job.
