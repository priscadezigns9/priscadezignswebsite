How to Pick the Perfect Carry-On

📅 2026 📖 5 min read ⭐ The Escapist

Your carry-on bag is the most important piece of travel gear you own. It travels with you on every trip, takes significant physical punishment, navigates tight overhead bins, and needs to meet airline size restrictions across every airline you fly. Getting this choice right has a compounding effect on every journey you take.

Size: Know the Rules

There is no universal carry-on size standard. Most full-service airlines accept approximately 55 x 40 x 20cm, but budget carriers can be considerably more restrictive. If you fly multiple airlines, size down rather than up. A slightly smaller bag that always fits is infinitely better than a perfectly sized bag that occasionally doesn't.

Hard Shell vs Soft Shell

Hard shell suitcases (polycarbonate or aluminium) offer better protection for fragile items, more resistance to external dirt and moisture, and the ability to lock securely. Their weakness is that they can't be compressed to fit into a slightly-too-small overhead bin. Soft shell bags are compressible when not fully packed, often have external access pockets, and weigh less.

Four-Wheel Spinners vs Two-Wheel Rollers

Four-wheel spinners can be rolled in any direction and are easy on smooth airport floors. Two-wheel rollers track in a straight line and handle rough terrain, cobblestones and outdoor surfaces far better. If you travel primarily through modern airports to hotel rooms, a spinner is convenient. If your travel involves rougher ground, a two-wheel roller is more practical.

Interior Organisation

The best carry-ons offer a main packing area, a separate laundry or shoe pocket, compression straps to secure clothing, and ideally a laptop pass-through or quick-access sleeve. Cross-body straps that align with security screening are particularly useful in airports with frequent checks.

Weight

If your airline counts carry-on weight, the empty weight of your bag matters. Many hard-shell spinners start at 3.5–4kg before you've packed a thing. Lightweight options come in under 3kg. Every hundred grams saved is a hundred grams you can fill with what you actually need.

Durability and Warranty

Handles, wheels and zips take enormous punishment. Invest in a bag from a brand that offers a meaningful warranty. Away, Rimowa, Samsonite, and Briggs and Riley all offer lifetime or multi-year guarantees covering manufacturing defects and in some cases travel damage. Cheap carry-ons rarely survive more than 30–40 trips before wheels or zips fail.

The Personal Item Strategy

Most airlines allow a personal item in addition to your carry-on. A structured backpack or day bag fits under the seat and provides quick-access storage for everything you need during the flight — headphones, snacks, documents, laptop. This two-bag strategy maximises your carry-on capacity and eliminates any need to open overhead bins mid-flight.

The perfect carry-on is one you stop thinking about — it fits where it needs to fit, opens easily, keeps everything organised, and survives every trip without complaint.
