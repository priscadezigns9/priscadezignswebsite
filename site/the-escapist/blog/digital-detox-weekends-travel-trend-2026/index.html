Unplugged: The Rise of Digital Detox Weekends in 2026

Published: May 23, 2026

In an increasingly connected world, the ultimate luxury in 2026 is becoming the ability to disconnect. According to the recently released Expedia Group 'Unpack '26' summer travel report, the most significant trend this year isn't a specific destination—it's the Digital Detox Weekend.

Why We're Unplugging

The report highlights a growing desire among travelers to reclaim their time and attention. Rather than seeking out "Instagrammable" moments, the 2026 traveler is looking for "presence." This shift is driving demand for domestic retreats that offer no-Wi-Fi zones, structured meditative activities, and immersion in natural environments. From the remote cabins of the Pacific Northwest to the hidden valleys of the Appalachian Mountains, the "off-grid" escape is the season's must-book trip.

Domestic Demand and the "Near-cation"

The "Unpack '26" data also shows a sharp rise in domestic travel. Travelers are opting for shorter, higher-quality trips closer to home, reducing travel time and environmental impact while maximizing relaxation. These "near-cations" allow for spontaneous weekend getaways that focus on high-touch experiences like farm-to-table dining and forest bathing.
