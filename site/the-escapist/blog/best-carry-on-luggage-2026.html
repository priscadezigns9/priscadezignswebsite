What Makes a Great Carry-On in 2026?

Three things dominate our scoring: dimension compliance (fitting every major airline's overhead limits), interior organisation (not just a cavern you stuff clothes into), and wheel quality (silent spinner wheels matter at 6am). Secondary factors include weight, durability, and whether the bag handles a month of weekly use without squeaking or cracking.

We tested each bag in real travel conditions — not on a smooth showroom floor. Cobblestones, jet bridges, and the notoriously aggressive baggage handlers at transit hubs all had a say in our final scores.

Pro tip: For international travel, aim for a bag no larger than 55 x 40 x 20cm. This clears restrictions at Ryanair, EasyJet, and most Asian budget carriers — the strictest in the industry.

Hard vs. Soft, Spinner vs. Roller: How to Choose

Hard vs. soft shell: Hardshell polycarbonate bags protect fragile items better and wipe clean in seconds. Softshell bags offer more flexibility when overstuffed and often weigh less. For most travellers, hardshell wins on three-day to one-week trips; softshell is better for trips where you might bring back more than you left with.

Two-wheel vs. four-wheel spinner: Four-wheel spinners dominate our top picks because they're easier to push through crowded terminals without lifting. Two-wheel rollaboards track better on uneven outdoor terrain — useful if you're hopping trains or navigating cobblestoned European streets.

TSA locks: TSA-approved locks allow airport security to open your bag without damaging it. If you travel internationally outside the TSA network, a standard combination lock is equally effective.

Size reminder: Always check your specific airline's carry-on policy before buying. United, Delta, and American allow 22 x 14 x 9 inches. International airlines — especially in Europe and Asia — are significantly stricter.
