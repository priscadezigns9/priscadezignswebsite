How to Pick the Perfect Carry-On Luggage

The right carry-on eliminates checked luggage fees, waiting at carousels, and the anxiety of lost bags. But choosing it requires understanding what matters and what doesn't.

Cabin Size Rules

Most airlines allow approximately 55x40x23cm (22x16x9 inches). This varies — budget airlines are stricter. If you fly multiple carriers, aim for the smaller end of this range. A bag that fits everywhere is worth more than a maximally sized bag that gets gate-checked regularly.

Hard Shell vs Soft Shell

Hard shell: better protection, easier to clean, looks sharper. Soft shell: expands slightly, external pockets for quick access, lighter. Hard shell wins for most travellers — the exterior pocket loss is outweighed by the durability and ease.

Wheel Configuration

Four spinner wheels (360-degree) vs two inline wheels. Spinners are effortless on smooth airport floors. Inline wheels handle cobblestones and kerbs better. For city travel, inline. For airport-to-airport, spinner.

Materials

Polycarbonate: lightweight, flexible, good impact resistance. Polypropylene: more rigid, slightly heavier. ABS: budget material, less durable. Polycarbonate is the sweet spot for most travellers.

TSA-Approved Lock

Built-in TSA combination locks allow security to open your bag without cutting the lock. Standard on most mid-range and premium bags.

Weight

An empty carry-on should weigh under 3kg. Every kg the bag weighs is a kg less you can pack within your airline's weight limit. Lighter shells and fewer external frames help here.
