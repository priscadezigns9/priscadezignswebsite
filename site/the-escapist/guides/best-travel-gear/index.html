Best Travel Gear and Accessories 2026

The difference between a stressful trip and a seamless one often comes down to what you packed. These are the travel accessories that consistently make the cut.

After thousands of miles of collective travel experience, a clear pattern emerges: the accessories that earn their place in the bag are the ones that solve specific pain points — lost luggage, dead batteries, aching necks, overstuffed suitcases. This list is built around those pain points.

We've organised by category. Pick what applies to your next trip and skip the rest.

The Non-Negotiable Carry-On Essentials

Carry-On

The Farpoint 40 is the benchmark carry-on backpack for serious travellers. 40 litres fits a week's worth of clothes for most trips. The panel-loading design means you access everything without digging. Internal frame stays comfortable even when it's full. TSA-compliant, airline carry-on friendly, and built to last decades. This is the last travel backpack you'll ever need to buy.

Instead of an inflatable ring that doesn't actually support your neck, it wraps like a scarf and provides rigid support exactly where your neck needs it. Lightweight, machine-washable, and considerably more comfortable than any traditional U-pillow for sleeping upright on planes.

Security & Connectivity

Security

Apple AirTag (4-Pack)

AirTags ended the luggage anxiety that comes with checked bags. Slip one in your suitcase, one in your backpack, and track them in real-time from your phone. When an airline loses your bag, you now know exactly where it is — a significant advantage in any baggage claim dispute. Works globally through the Find My network. Works with non-Apple phone via web interface.

The Perfect Packing List

  • Travel backpack or carry-on
  • Packing cubes (4-set)
  • Power bank 20,000mAh
  • Universal travel adapter
  • Noise-cancelling headphones
  • AirTags or tracking tags
  • Neck pillow (Trtl or similar)
  • Compression socks for flights
  • TSA-approved toiletry bottles
  • Microfibre travel towel
  • Slim travel wallet/RFID blocker
  • Day bag / packable daypack
The One-Bag Philosophy: If you can fit everything in a 40L carry-on, do it. You'll never wait at baggage claim again, never lose a bag again, and never pay checked baggage fees again. Three weeks in South East Asia fits in a properly packed 40L with space to spare.
