How to Build Client Websites
That Actually Convert

May 2025  ·  6 min read

← Back to Blog

Most client websites look great in the portfolio and underperform in the real world. The gap between beautiful and effective is almost always structural. Here's how to close it.

Start With One Goal

Before you open any design tool, ask the client: what is the single most important action a visitor should take? Book a call? Buy a product? Fill out a form? Everything on the page should serve that one goal. The moment you add a second goal, conversion rates drop. Most sites fail because they try to do too much — part brochure, part portfolio, part shop, part blog. Pick the primary objective and build around it ruthlessly.

The Hero Section Does 80% of the Work

Visitors decide in under 5 seconds whether they're staying. The hero needs to answer three questions instantly:

  • What is this? (Clear headline, no jargon)
  • Who is it for? (Speak directly to the target customer)
  • What should I do next? (One CTA, above the fold, impossible to miss)

If your hero answers all three, the rest of the page is just supporting evidence.

Social Proof Belongs Near the CTA

Don't bury testimonials at the bottom. Place them directly adjacent to your main call to action — above the fold if possible, or immediately below the first CTA button. The moment of hesitation is when social proof does its job. Time it accordingly.

Speed Is a Conversion Factor

A one-second delay in page load time reduces conversions by up to 7%. Compress images before uploading, use system fonts where possible, and avoid loading scripts that aren't essential to the page's primary function.

Mobile Is the Primary Screen

Design mobile-first, then adapt for desktop — not the other way around. More than 60% of web traffic is mobile. If the CTA is buried below the fold on a phone, or the form fields are too small to tap, you're losing conversions before the visitor reads your copy.

Test One Variable at a Time

Once the site is live, the work isn't done. Run simple A/B tests: headline A vs B, button colour, CTA copy. Change one thing at a time. Over 90 days, iterative testing compounds into significant conversion improvements.
