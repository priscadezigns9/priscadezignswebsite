From Queries to Agents: The 2026 Architectural Shift of the Internet

At Google I/O 2026, Sundar Pichai dropped what many are calling the biggest architectural shift to the internet since the 1990s. We are officially moving from a **'Query-based' Internet** to an **'Agent-based' Internet**. For web developers at Velloq, this changes everything. In the query-based era, users searched for information and navigated to sites to complete tasks. In the agent-based era, AI agents—like the new Gemini 3.5 Flash—will navigate the web on behalf of the user. This means websites must now be optimized not just for human eyes and SEO, but for 'Agent Readability' and autonomous interaction. This shift requires a fundamental redesign of how we build web applications. APIs, structured data, and serverless architecture are more important than ever. The ability for an agent to successfully complete a checkout process or book a service without human intervention will be the new benchmark for a successful website in 2026. As we embrace this new era, Velloq is at the forefront of building 'Agent-Ready' platforms. The internet is no longer just a place to find information; it's a place where work gets done by autonomous intelligence. The future of web development is here, and it's agentic.
