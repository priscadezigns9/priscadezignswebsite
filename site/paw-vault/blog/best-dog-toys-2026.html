The Best Dog Toys to Keep
Your Pup Entertained in 2026

Published May 13, 2026 By the Paw Vault Team

Bored dogs are destructive dogs. A dog who doesn't get adequate mental and physical stimulation will find stimulation — usually in your couch cushions, your shoes, or your garbage can. The right toys aren't a luxury; they're a management tool and a welfare necessity.

This guide covers the best dog toys of 2026 across every category — chewers, smart dogs, anxious dogs, outdoor dogs, and puppies. We've tested or sourced dozens of options, and these are the ones that genuinely hold up.

How to Rotate Toys Properly

Dogs get bored with the same toys on offer every day. The solution is rotation: divide your dog's toys into 3–4 groups and rotate access weekly. A toy your dog hasn't seen in a week becomes exciting again. This keeps a modest toy collection feeling new indefinitely and prevents the "too many toys, interested in none" problem.
