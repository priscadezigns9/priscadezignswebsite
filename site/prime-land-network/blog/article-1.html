5 Home Improvements That Add the Most Value

Most home improvements cost more than they add in value. These five are the exceptions — they pay back more than they cost and make properties sell faster.

1. Kitchen Refresh (Not Full Renovation)

A full kitchen renovation rarely recoups its cost. But cabinet repainting, new hardware, updated lighting, and a new splashback can transform a kitchen for under $3,000 and add $10,000-20,000 in perceived value.

2. Bathroom Updates

New taps, a re-grouted shower, updated lighting, and a fresh vanity make a bathroom feel current without full renovation costs. Buyers assess bathrooms closely — dated fixtures are a significant negative signal.

3. Kerb Appeal

The first impression determines the emotional response that shapes every subsequent judgment. A repainted front door, clean driveway, and maintained garden cost relatively little and have outsized impact on both sale price and time on market.

4. Smart Home Basics

A smart lock, video doorbell, and smart thermostat cost $300-600 combined and signal a modern, well-maintained property. Buyers in 2026 expect these features and notice their absence.

5. Energy Efficiency Upgrades

LED lighting throughout, improved insulation, and a modern boiler or HVAC system reduce running costs and increasingly command premiums in markets where energy prices are high. These are also increasingly required for mortgage eligibility in some markets.
