Smart Home Devices Worth Buying in 2026

Smart home technology has matured. The gimmicks have been separated from the genuinely useful. Here's what's actually worth adding to your home in 2026.

Video Doorbell

A video doorbell lets you see, speak to, and record whoever is at your door from anywhere in the world. Package theft prevention, security footage, and remote access to deliveries make this the highest practical-value smart home purchase for most households.

Smart Lock

Keyless entry via app, PIN, or fingerprint. Share temporary access codes with cleaners or guests. Monitor who enters and when. The convenience is real — the security concern (that smart locks are hackable) is largely theoretical versus the practical daily benefit.

Smart Thermostat

Learning thermostats (Nest, Ecobee) reduce energy consumption by 10-23% on average by learning your schedule and adjusting automatically. They pay for themselves within 18-24 months in most climates.

Smart Plugs

The lowest-cost, highest-flexibility smart home entry point. Turn any appliance into a smart device. Schedule, automate, and monitor energy usage. A pack of four costs $25-40.

Security Camera (Indoor/Outdoor)

Outdoor cameras with motion detection, night vision, and cloud storage provide genuine security value. Indoor cameras are useful for pet monitoring and package placement tracking.

What to Skip

Smart fridges, smart mirrors, and many voice-controlled kitchen appliances remain expensive novelties with limited practical benefit. Focus on devices that solve real daily friction points.
