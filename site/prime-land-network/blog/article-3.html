How to Stage a Home for a Fast Sale

Staged homes sell faster and for more money. Professional staging costs $1,500-5,000. But 80% of the impact comes from actions that cost very little. Here's what actually matters.

Declutter First, Always

Remove 30-50% of everything in the home. This is the single highest-impact action in staging. Buyers need to see space — they cannot visualise their life in a cluttered home. Hire a storage unit for the duration of the sale if needed.

Deep Clean

A professionally cleaned home signals that it has been well-maintained. Pay particular attention to kitchens, bathrooms, windows, and floors. Smells matter — avoid strong cooking odours and pet smells on viewing days.

Neutral Colours

Repaint bold or personalised colour schemes in neutral whites or warm greys. Buyers need to see themselves in the space — a bright orange feature wall makes that harder. Neutral doesn't mean boring — it means inclusive.

Furniture Arrangement

Every room should have one clear purpose and a logical flow. Remove excess furniture. In living rooms, float sofas away from walls — it makes rooms feel larger. In bedrooms, ensure you can walk around both sides of the bed.

Lighting

Maximise natural light (remove heavy curtains, clean windows, trim outdoor plants blocking windows). Add warm artificial light where natural light is limited. Dark corners make spaces feel smaller and unwelcoming.

Finishing Touches

Fresh flowers, new white towels in bathrooms, a bowl of fruit in the kitchen, and a made bed with neutral bedding cost under $100 and create the emotional response that makes buyers feel at home.
