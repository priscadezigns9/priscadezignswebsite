FERRARI LUCE

The electric supercar era has arrived at Maranello. Designed in collaboration with Jony Ive and Marc Newson’s LoveFrom, the Ferrari Luce is a radical departure from the aerodynamic obsession of the last decade.

THE ANALOG REBELLION

In a world of glass cockpits, the Luce makes a shocking statement: zero touchscreens. The interior features a purely tactile interface. Knurled aluminum dials, mechanical toggles, and a physical "Manettino" that feels like a precision time object.

"Design is not just how it looks. It's how it works—and in the Luce, it works through friction, resistance, and the weight of Italian engineering."

SPECIFICATIONS OF THE VOID

Under the carbon-fiber skin lies a four-motor configuration delivering a staggering 1,114 horsepower. The battery pack, integrated as a structural element, enables a 0-62 mph sprint in just 1.9 seconds. But the numbers are secondary to the sound—a synthesized, resonant frequency that captures the "soul" of a V12.

The Autodrome Lab will be tracking the first private delivery slots this June.
