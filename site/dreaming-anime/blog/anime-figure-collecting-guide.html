How to Start Collecting Anime Figures: Complete Beginner Guide

Anime figure collecting is one of the most rewarding hobbies in the fandom — but it's easy to waste money buying the wrong things when you're starting out. This guide covers everything you need to know before spending a single dollar.

Understanding Figure Grades and Prices

Scale Figures (1/7 or 1/8 scale) — the premium tier. Produced by Good Smile Company, Alter, Kotobukiya, Max Factory. Price range: $80-$400. These are display-grade with incredible detail and paint work. Prize Figures — won from UFO catchers in Japanese arcades, now sold online. Produced by Taito, Banpresto, Furyu. Price range: $15-$50. Great value but lower detail. Nendoroids and Figmas — small, chibi-style or articulated figures by Good Smile. Range: $50-$90. Highly collectible and great for beginners.

Where to Buy Legitimate Figures

Always buy from: Crunchyroll Store, Right Stuf Anime, AmiAmi, BigBadToyStore, or directly from manufacturers. Avoid: random Amazon third-party sellers, wish.com, Temu. Bootleg figures look convincing in photos but have terrible paint quality and sometimes contain toxic materials. A real figure listing will always have a manufacturer and release date.

Pre-ordering and Aftermarket Strategy

Most high-quality figures sell out at retail and never restock. Pre-ordering directly from AmiAmi, GSC, or CDJapan at retail price is the smart play. Aftermarket prices on MyFigureCollection (MFC) or Mandarake can run 2-5x retail within a year. Check MFC for historical prices before buying aftermarket — some figures appreciate dramatically, others don't.

Display and Care Tips

Keep figures out of direct sunlight — UV rays yellow plastic and fade paint. Use a glass display case with a door to prevent dust accumulation. Clean with a soft brush or canned air, never wet cloths. Detach accessories during long-term storage to prevent stress marks. For rare figures, keep original boxes — they add significant resale value.
