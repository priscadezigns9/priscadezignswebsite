How to Clean Sneakers Like a Pro

Cleaning sneakers incorrectly causes more damage than dirt does. The wrong products, wrong techniques, and wrong order of operations can ruin soles, distort uppers, and destroy materials that took years to acquire. Here's how professionals do it.

Know Your Material First

The cleaning method depends entirely on the upper material. Leather, suede, canvas, mesh, and knit all require different approaches. Applying a leather cleaner to suede, for example, can permanently damage the nap. Identify what you're working with before you touch anything.

Tools You Need

  • Soft-bristle brush (for uppers and laces)
  • Medium-bristle brush (for midsoles)
  • Stiff brush (for outsoles)
  • Microfiber cloths
  • Bowl of clean water
  • Dedicated sneaker cleaning solution (not dish soap — it strips protective finishes)

The Correct Order

Remove and clean laces separately. Clean the outsole first (most dirt). Then the midsole. Then the upper last — working on a clean platform ensures you don't drag outsole contamination onto the upper.

Leather Uppers

Dampen a soft brush, apply cleaner, work in small circular motions. Wipe clean with a microfiber cloth. Condition after cleaning — leather loses moisture during the cleaning process. Skip conditioning and the leather will crack over time.

Suede and Nubuck

Dry brush first to loosen surface dirt. Use a suede-specific cleaner and brush with the nap (not against it). Never use water on suede without a water-resistant treatment applied first. A suede eraser handles scuffs better than liquid cleaners.

Mesh and Knit

Dilute your cleaner further for mesh. Light circular motions with a soft brush. Stuff the shoe with paper towels before cleaning to maintain shape. Air dry only — heat warps the structure.

Drying

Always air dry at room temperature. Never use a dryer, radiator, or direct sunlight — heat damages adhesives, warps soles, and cracks leather. Stuff with paper (not newspaper — ink transfers) to maintain shape while drying.
