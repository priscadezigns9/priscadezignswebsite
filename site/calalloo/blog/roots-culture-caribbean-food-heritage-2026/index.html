Roots & Culture: Reconnecting with Caribbean Food Heritage in Summer 2026

This May 2026, the Caribbean food scene is looking backward to move forward. The **'Roots & Culture'** movement is sweeping the islands, as a new generation of chefs and home cooks reconnects with their ancestry and the 'Ourstory' of Caribbean cuisine. The trend focuses on heritage ingredients that were once overlooked—roots like dasheen, yam, and cassava—and elevating them into modern, gourmet dishes. It's about honoring the resilience and creativity of our ancestors who took simple, local ingredients and turned them into the vibrant, flavorful dishes we love today. At Calalloo, we're celebrating this movement by highlighting traditional recipes and the stories behind them. From the proper way to prepare a peltate dasheen leaf for a rich soup to the secrets of authentic Caribbean seasonings, 'Roots & Culture' is about preserving our culinary heritage in a fast-paced, digital world. As we move into the summer of 2026, expect to see more 'farm-to-table' pop-ups and heritage food festivals across the region. Caribbean food is not just about taste; it's about identity, and this year, that identity is stronger than ever.
