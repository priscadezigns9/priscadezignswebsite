Skincare Routine Order for Beginners 2026

Apply in the wrong order and your products cancel each other out. Apply them right, and your skin transforms in weeks. Here's exactly what to do and when.

Most skincare mistakes aren't about which products you're using — they're about the order you're applying them. Layering moisturiser before serum, or SPF before sunscreen-blocking actives, essentially undoes the benefits you paid for. This guide fixes that.

We'll cover the morning routine, the evening routine, and the science behind why the order matters — plus the exact products we recommend for each step.

Why Order Matters in Skincare

Skincare products work by penetrating into different layers of skin. The general rule is: apply thinnest to thickest. Water-based serums need direct skin contact to absorb. Heavier moisturisers and oils create a seal on top. If you apply them in the wrong order, the lighter product can't get through.

Active ingredients like retinol, vitamin C, and AHAs/BHAs are also pH-sensitive. If you layer them in the wrong sequence, you can neutralise their effectiveness entirely.

Morning Routine — Step by Step

1

Cleanser

Start with a gentle, pH-balanced cleanser. In the morning, you're washing off sweat and the residue from your night products — not heavy-duty dirt — so a gentle formula is fine. Avoid foaming cleansers with sulfates if you have dry or sensitive skin.

2

Toner (Optional)

If you use a toner, apply it on damp skin immediately after cleansing. Modern toners are hydrating, not astringent — look for ingredients like hyaluronic acid, niacinamide, or glycerin. Skip if your skin is already feeling balanced.

3

Vitamin C Serum

Vitamin C is your morning antioxidant. Applied before SPF, it boosts sun protection and works to brighten and even skin tone throughout the day. Apply 2–3 drops and press into skin — don't rub. Wait 30 seconds before the next step.

4

Moisturiser

Lock in your serum and hydrate your skin barrier. Choose based on your skin type: gel moisturisers for oily/combo skin, cream for dry, lotion for normal. Apply while skin is still slightly damp from the previous step for better absorption.

5

SPF — Non-Negotiable

SPF is the most important step in any skincare routine, full stop. UV damage is the #1 cause of premature ageing, dark spots, and skin cancer. Use SPF 30 minimum, SPF 50 preferred. Apply this last — nothing goes over SPF. Nothing.

Evening Routine — Step by Step

1

Double Cleanse

Start with an oil-based cleanser (micellar water or cleansing balm) to dissolve SPF and makeup, then follow with your regular gel or cream cleanser. This two-step approach guarantees a truly clean canvas for your actives.

2

Exfoliant (2–3x/week only)

AHAs (glycolic, lactic acid) or BHAs (salicylic acid) go here. Don't use daily — your skin barrier needs recovery time. AHAs are better for dry/normal skin and brightness; BHAs penetrate pores and are ideal for oily or acne-prone skin.

3

Treatment Serum

Retinol, niacinamide, or peptides go here. If you're using retinol, start with once a week and build up to avoid irritation. Never use retinol with AHAs/BHAs on the same night — that combination is too harsh for most skin types.

4

Eye Cream (Optional)

The eye area is thinner and more sensitive. If you use an eye cream, apply it before your main moisturiser using your ring finger (lightest pressure). Pat, don't rub.

5

Night Moisturiser / Sleeping Mask

Your skin repairs itself while you sleep. A richer night cream or sleeping mask seals in all your actives and maximises overnight recovery. If you're using retinol, a barrier-supporting moisturiser with ceramides here is essential.
