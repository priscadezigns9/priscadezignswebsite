What Are Peptides and Why Do They Work?

Peptides are short chains of amino acids — the building blocks of proteins in the skin, including collagen, elastin, and keratin. When applied topically, specific peptide sequences signal the skin to produce more collagen, repair its barrier, improve firmness, and enhance hydration. Unlike retinol, which achieves anti-aging results through cellular turnover that can cause irritation, sensitivity, and purging, peptides work by communicating with the skin's natural processes. The result: measurable improvements in elasticity, fine lines, and skin quality without the inflammation that makes retinol incompatible with many skin types.

How to Incorporate Peptides Into Your 2026 Skincare Routine

Peptides are highly compatible with most other active ingredients — a significant advantage over retinol, which requires careful layering protocols. The general principle: apply peptide serums to cleansed, slightly damp skin before heavier moisturizers. They can be used morning and evening without sensitivity concerns in most formulations. For those transitioning from retinol, consider a peptide-forward PM routine while maintaining retinol 2-3 nights per week for the cell turnover benefits.
