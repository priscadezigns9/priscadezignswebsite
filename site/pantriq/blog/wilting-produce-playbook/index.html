The Wilting Produce Playbook: 12 Meals You Can Make Before It Goes Bad

May 2025 • 7 min read • By Pantriq

That soft courgette in the corner of the fridge. The spinach on its last day. The tomatoes that are just past their prime for salads but absolutely fine for cooking. Every kitchen has them. Most of them get thrown away because dinner planning is already done and there's no room for improvisation.

This playbook gives you 12 specific meals, grouped by vegetable type, that you can build in under 25 minutes from produce that's starting to turn. Consider it a rescue system — and the difference between a $40 food shop that delivers full value and one that wastes $12.

The golden rule

Soft vegetables don't need to be crisp to be good — they need heat. Roasting, sauteing, and simmering transform limp produce into deeply flavoured food. Use texture loss as a nudge to cook, not a reason to bin.

Soft Spinach or Leafy Greens

Creamed Spinach Pasta

Wilt spinach in butter with garlic, add cream or any milk, season, toss with cooked pasta. Done in 15 minutes. The spinach flavour actually intensifies slightly when wilted.

Green Omelette

Chop spinach roughly and fold into a 3-egg omelette with cheese. Wilted leaves distribute more evenly than fresh ones and create better texture in eggs.

Lentil & Greens Soup

Simmer red lentils with onion, garlic, and stock for 20 minutes. Add spinach in the last 3 minutes. Season with cumin and lemon. A complete meal that uses up a full bag of greens.

Soft Courgette / Zucchini

Courgette Fritters

Grate courgette, squeeze out moisture, mix with 1 egg, 2 tbsp flour, salt, and herbs. Pan-fry in patties until golden. Serve with yogurt or hot sauce.

Roasted Courgette Pasta Sauce

Dice courgette with garlic and cherry tomatoes. Roast at 200°C for 20 minutes. Toss with pasta, olive oil, and parmesan. Soft courgette roasts faster and develops better colour.

Quick Ratatouille

Dice courgette with any other soft veg (peppers, tomatoes, onion). Saute with olive oil and garlic. Simmer 15 minutes with a tin of tomatoes. Serve with bread or rice.

Overripe Tomatoes

Shakshuka

Dice and cook tomatoes with onion and peppers until they break down. Create wells and crack in 3-4 eggs. Cover and simmer until eggs are set. 20 minutes, zero waste, deeply satisfying.

Quick Tomato Sauce (Batch)

Cook all soft tomatoes down with olive oil, garlic, salt, and basil for 25 minutes. Blend. Freeze in portions. One pot of sauce from produce you nearly threw away.

Tomato Soup

Roast tomatoes with garlic and onion for 20 minutes. Blend with stock and a little cream. Season. The roasting compensates for lost freshness with concentrated flavour.

Soft Peppers

Pepper & Egg Scramble

Saute thin slices of soft pepper in butter or oil. Add beaten eggs and scramble. Season with salt, pepper, and chilli flakes. 8 minutes total.

Roasted Pepper Dip

Roast peppers whole under a grill until blackened. Peel, blend with olive oil, garlic, and a little vinegar. A completely usable condiment or dip that lasts 5 days in the fridge.

Pepper & Chickpea Stew

Dice and saute peppers with onion and garlic. Add a tin of chickpeas and a tin of tomatoes. Simmer 20 minutes with cumin and paprika. Serve over rice or with bread.

Build the Habit

Set a weekly "use it up" session — 30 minutes on Thursday or Friday before your next shop. Open the fridge, assess what's close to the edge, and match it to this playbook. Over time you'll build personal recipes around what your household tends to let slide, and your food waste will drop significantly.

Every ingredient you rescue is money back in your pocket. The playbook is just the nudge to do it.
