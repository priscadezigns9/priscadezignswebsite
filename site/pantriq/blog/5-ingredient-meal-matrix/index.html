The 5-Ingredient Meal Matrix: How to Plan a Full Week From One Shopping Trip

May 2025 • 6 min read • By Pantriq

The average household throws away nearly a third of the food it buys. Not because they're careless — because they're under-planned. A spontaneous Tuesday grocery run becomes a bag of impulse purchases that never quite comes together into a meal. Sound familiar?

The 5-Ingredient Meal Matrix is a simple planning framework that fixes this. By selecting 5 core ingredients that cross-pollinate across multiple meals, you can turn a single weekly shop into 5 varied, satisfying dinners with virtually no waste.

The Core Principle

Most meals share building blocks. Chicken thighs can become a stir-fry, a soup, a tray bake, or tacos. Canned tomatoes underpin pasta sauce, shakshuka, and chilli. When you plan around ingredients rather than individual recipes, you stop buying things that only appear in one meal and disappear into the back of the fridge.

The matrix works by selecting:

  • 1 protein (chicken, eggs, lentils, tofu)
  • 1 starch (rice, pasta, potatoes, bread)
  • 2 versatile vegetables (onion + spinach, peppers + courgette, etc.)
  • 1 flavour base (garlic, ginger, miso, tomato paste)

Plus your pantry staples: oil, salt, spices, and condiments you already own.

An Example Matrix: Chicken + Rice + Onion + Peppers + Garlic

Monday: Garlic Rice Bowl

Cook rice, fry garlic and onion, slice leftover raw chicken into strips and pan-fry. Season with soy sauce and serve over rice with sliced peppers.

Tuesday: Roasted Pepper & Chicken Tray Bake

Quarter the remaining chicken (thighs work best). Toss with sliced peppers, onion, garlic, olive oil, salt, and any dried herbs. Roast at 200°C for 35 minutes. Serve with rice.

Wednesday: Simple Chicken Rice Soup

Use the chicken carcass (if applicable) or a portion of cooked chicken. Simmer with onion, garlic, and water for 20 minutes. Add rice, season, done. A genuinely good weeknight meal in 25 minutes.

Thursday: Pepper & Egg Fried Rice

Day-old rice fries better. Scramble 2 eggs into the wok first, add cold rice, diced peppers and onion, and garlic. Season with soy sauce and sesame oil. Full meal in 12 minutes.

Friday: Stuffed Peppers

Halve remaining peppers. Fill with cooked rice, diced onion, garlic, any remaining chicken, and a can of tomatoes (pantry staple). Bake 25 minutes. Impressive, minimal effort, and everything gets used.

How to Build Your Own Matrix

  1. Pick your 5 ingredients using the framework above.
  2. Before shopping, plan 4-5 meals on paper using only those 5 ingredients + pantry items.
  3. Write the shopping list from the meals, not the other way around.
  4. Don't add to the list just because it "sounds nice" — if it doesn't appear in a planned meal, it becomes waste.

"The goal isn't eating the same thing five nights. The goal is buying five things that can become five different experiences."

What to Do With Leftover Portions

The matrix deliberately generates small surpluses. A few leaves of spinach, half an onion, the last spoonful of garlic — these belong in Friday's omelette, Saturday's lunch wrap, or Sunday's brunch scramble. Plan those two extra meals in advance and your food waste drops to near zero.

The best meal planners aren't people who spend two hours on a Sunday cooking batch meals. They're people who buy smart and bridge the gap between ingredients and meals creatively. The matrix gives you the structure to do exactly that.
