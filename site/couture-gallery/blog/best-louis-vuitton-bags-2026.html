The Best Louis Vuitton Bags Worth Buying in 2026

Louis Vuitton produces hundreds of bag styles — but not all of them hold their value, wear well over time, or justify their price. These are the bags that do all three.

The Neverfull: The Workhorse Classic

The Neverfull MM in Monogram or Damier Ebene is the definitive LV purchase for a reason. It fits a 13-inch laptop, expands and cinches via side laces, comes with a removable pouch, and has been in production since 2007 with no signs of discontinuation. Retail is around $1,600-$1,900. Resale on StockX and Vestiaire routinely exceeds retail for newer models.

The Speedy 25 and 30: The Heritage Choice

The Speedy is the original Louis Vuitton handbag design, dating to 1930. The 25 fits comfortably on the arm; the 30 accommodates a full day's essentials. Both are available in Monogram and Damier. The Speedy Bandoulière adds a shoulder strap for versatility. At $1,200-$1,500 retail, it's the most accessible entry into heritage LV.

The Pochette Accessories: The Smart Entry Point

At $700-$800 retail, the Pochette Accessories is the best value in the LV line. Wear as a clutch, attach to the Neverfull's D-ring, or use as a mini crossbody with the optional strap. It's consistently selling above retail on the secondary market — a rare feat for an entry-level piece.

2026 Investment Picks: New Releases to Watch

The LV Loop Bag in the new Taurillon Monogram leather is the breakout 2026 release — structured, versatile, and already waitlisted. The Capucines BB in Magnolia pink sold out immediately on LV.com and is trading at 40% premium on resale. Both are likely to appreciate meaningfully if kept in excellent condition.
