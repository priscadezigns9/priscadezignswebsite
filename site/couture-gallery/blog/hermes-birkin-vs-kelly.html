Hermès Birkin vs Kelly: Which Is the Better Investment?

The Birkin and the Kelly are the two most coveted handbags in the world — and owning either requires patience, a spending history at Hermès, and significant capital. Here's an honest comparison of which is the better investment in 2026.

The Birkin: What You Need to Know

The Birkin was created in 1984 after Jane Birkin complained on a flight to Jean-Louis Dumas about difficulty finding a suitable weekend bag. Available in sizes 25, 30, 35, and 40cm. The Birkin 25 in Togo leather in a neutral colour (Gold, Etoupe, Black) is the most liquid resale item in luxury — routinely reselling at 150-200% of retail. Retail is €8,000-€14,000 depending on size and leather; resale runs €20,000-€50,000 for in-demand combinations.

The Kelly: The Underrated Investment

The Kelly predates the Birkin, originating in the 1930s as the Sac à dépêches. Available in Retourne (relaxed, softer) or Sellier (structured, formal) construction in sizes 25, 28, 32, and 35cm. The Kelly 28 Sellier in Box or Barenia leather is arguably the more elegant of the two bags — and more practical with its crossbody strap. It historically appreciated slightly less than the Birkin but that gap has closed significantly since 2022.

Investment Performance 2020-2026

Knight Frank's Luxury Investment Index shows Hermès handbags outperformed gold, art, and wine over the past decade. Birkin 25 in Togo Gold: +220% over 10 years. Kelly 28 Sellier in Black Epsom: +180% over 10 years. Both dramatically outperform any savings account. Exotic leathers (crocodile, ostrich) carry higher volatility but also higher ceiling returns.

Which Should You Buy?

If resale liquidity is the priority: Birkin 25 or 30 in Togo, neutral colour. It sells faster and at higher premiums than any other single luxury item. If personal style is the priority: the Kelly is the more elegant, versatile, and underappreciated choice — better for formal occasions, more distinctive, and increasingly sought after by serious collectors.
