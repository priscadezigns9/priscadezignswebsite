EDITORIAL SPOTLIGHT — DIVE WATCHES

Why the Submariner
Remains Untouchable.

Since 1953, the Rolex Submariner has set the standard against which every dive watch — and arguably every sport watch — is measured. It is the reference. Not because Rolex says so, but because the market, the collectors, the culture have all arrived at the same verdict, independently, over seven decades. The Submariner doesn't compete. It defines the category.

What makes it untouchable is not specs — it's convergence. The Submariner is simultaneously the first serious dive watch, the Bond watch, the street-style icon, the investment-grade timepiece, and the benchmark by which enthusiasts calibrate everything else. No other watch on earth occupies all five positions at once. To own one is to hold the distilled history of modern watchmaking. That doesn't depreciate.
