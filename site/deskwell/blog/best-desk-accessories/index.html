The Only 12 Desk Accessories Worth Buying (And 8 to Skip)

May 2025 • 8 min read • By Deskwell

The desk accessory market has exploded. There's a gadget for everything, a solution for every "problem" — including problems you didn't know you had until you saw an ad for the solution. Most of it is noise.

After testing dozens of products and surveying hundreds of productive workspace setups, we've distilled the list down to what actually matters. Here's the honest, no-affiliate-bias breakdown.

The 12 Worth It

01

Monitor Arm (Single or Dual)

Frees your entire desk surface, enables precise monitor positioning for ergonomics, and looks clean. Get one with full range of motion. Ergotron LX is the benchmark — worth every penny.

02

Cable Management Tray + Clips

Visible cables create visual noise that costs cognitive bandwidth. A $15 cable tray under the desk + adhesive clips on the back of your monitor arm transforms the look and feel of any setup.

03

Wrist Rest (Keyboard)

Only matters if you type heavily. Memory foam over gel — it doesn't compress unevenly over time. Not a permanent resting place — a cushion for micro-breaks between bursts of typing.

04

Desk Pad / Deskmat

Unifies the look of your setup, protects the surface, and gives your mouse a consistent glide surface. Large (800mm+) leather or fabric options from Orbitkey, Grovemade, or Amazon Basics all work well.

05

USB-C Docking Hub (7-in-1 minimum)

One cable in, everything out. Essential for laptop users. Anker, Plugable, and CalDigit make reliable options at different price points. The CalDigit TS4 is the professional standard.

06

Laptop Stand + External Keyboard

If you use a laptop as your main machine, a stand brings the screen to eye level. Pair with a compact mechanical keyboard for the right input ergonomics. The Nexstand K2 folds flat — travels well.

07

Bias Lighting Strip

An LED strip behind your monitor reduces eye strain by narrowing the contrast between screen brightness and ambient darkness. Nanoleaf and Govee both make sync-capable options that work with video content.

08

Physical Inbox Tray

A single place for incoming paper, notes, and items that need processing. Two-tiered: Inbox (unsorted) and Action (needs to happen today). This simple system keeps your Zone 1 permanently clear.

09

Mechanical Keyboard

Even a mid-range mechanical keyboard ($70–$120) dramatically improves typing accuracy, speed, and tactile feedback vs. membrane alternatives. Keychron K2 or K8 are excellent entry points.

10

Ergonomic Mouse (Vertical or Standard)

For mouse-heavy work (design, editing, browsing), a vertical mouse reduces wrist pronation and repetitive strain. Logitech MX Vertical or the standard MX Master 3 are both excellent depending on usage.

11

Small Whiteboard or Notepad (Physical)

Keep a 10x15cm whiteboard or sticky pad in your capture zone. Fastest tool for offloading the "don't forget this" thought mid-deep-work session without opening a new app window.

12

One Quality Desk Lamp

Directed task lighting, separate from overhead, prevents fatigue during evening sessions. The BenQ e-Reading Lamp with automatic colour temperature adjustment is worth the price. Budget option: any adjustable arm lamp with a 4000K bulb.

The 8 to Skip

Desk organisers with 12+ compartments

They fill up with junk immediately and make clutter look organised. Use a single drawer or one small tray instead.

Wireless charging pads (as primary setup)

Slow, position-sensitive, and get in the way. A short fast-charging cable does the job better in every way.

Gadget charging stations (the big ones)

Adds bulk and visual noise to Zone 1. Keep charging out of your workspace entirely if you can.

Fidget toys and "stress relievers"

These address symptoms (restlessness, anxiety) rather than root causes (poor breaks, bad ergonomics, too-long sessions).

Mini desktop fans

Usually underpowered and noisy. Proper ventilation in your room is the fix, not a desk gadget.

Motivational quote plaques

Visual clutter that contributes nothing functional. If you need motivation from an object, address that at the source.

Overpriced "aesthetic" pen holders

You need 2-3 pens accessible. A simple cup works. The $80 marble pen holder does not improve your output.

Ring lights (for non-video users)

Unless you're on video calls for 3+ hours daily, a ring light is overkill. A good desk lamp with the right Kelvin does the same job more flexibly.

The 12-Word Rule

Before any desk accessory purchase, ask: does this solve a real friction point, or does it just look good on a setup tour? If you can't name the specific problem it solves, don't buy it. Your desk will thank you.
