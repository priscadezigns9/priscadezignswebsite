Monitor Arms:
Do You Really Need One?

Published May 16, 2026 · By the Deskwell Editorial Team

A monitor arm is one of the most-asked-about desk accessories. The honest answer: not everyone needs one — but the people who do need one will feel the difference immediately. Here's how to know which camp you're in, and our top picks for 2026.

Walk into any serious home office or tech workspace and you'll see monitor arms everywhere. They've become shorthand for "I take my setup seriously" — and for good reason. But the case for them isn't purely aesthetic.

Monitor arms solve three real problems: poor monitor positioning (the leading cause of neck strain for desk workers), wasted desk surface area (a monitor stand can occupy 15–25cm of depth that an arm reclaims entirely), and lack of flexibility (arms let you swing the monitor out of the way when you need the full desk surface).

The Honest Case For and Against

✓ Reasons to Get One

  • Perfect monitor height adjustment — prevents neck strain
  • Reclaims 15–25cm of desk depth
  • Infinitely adjustable: tilt, pan, rotate
  • Cable management built in on quality arms
  • Works for portrait mode coding setups
  • Enables true dual-monitor configurations

✗ Reasons to Skip

  • Expensive quality arms cost $120–200
  • Requires a VESA-compatible monitor (most modern ones are)
  • Clamp damages thin desk edges
  • Unnecessary if you already have correct monitor height
  • Over-engineered for occasional users

Who Should Definitely Get One

Get a monitor arm if: (1) you experience neck or upper back pain at the end of work days, (2) your current monitor position forces you to look down more than 15 degrees, (3) your monitor stand takes up more than 20cm of desk depth that you could use for something else, or (4) you work with two monitors and want them in a clean side-by-side or portrait-plus-landscape configuration.

Skip it if: you use a laptop primarily (a laptop stand serves better), you're a casual user who works 2–3 hours at most, or your current monitor setup is already at the right height with no strain.
