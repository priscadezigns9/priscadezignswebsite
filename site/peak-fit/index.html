Frequently Asked Questions

How do I start working out at home?

Begin with 3 bodyweight sessions per week: push/pull/legs split. Invest in resistance bands and a mat first. Progress to dumbbells once you've built the habit (6–8 weeks).

What equipment do I need for a home gym?

Minimum viable gym: adjustable dumbbells, pull-up bar, resistance bands, and a mat ($200–400 total). Advanced: add a power rack and barbell for $800–1,200.

How many days a week should I train?

Beginners: 3 days. Intermediate: 4 days. Advanced: 5–6 days. Recovery is where muscle is built — never skip rest days.

What is progressive overload?

Systematically increasing training stress over time — more weight, more reps, or less rest. It's the single most important principle for continuous improvement.

How long until I see fitness results?

Strength gains: 2–3 weeks. Visible muscle changes: 6–8 weeks. Significant body composition change: 3–6 months. Consistency beats intensity every time.

What is the best protein supplement?

Whey protein isolate is the gold standard for absorption and amino acid profile. For plant-based, pea + rice blend matches whey's results. Aim for 0.7–1g of protein per lb of bodyweight daily.
