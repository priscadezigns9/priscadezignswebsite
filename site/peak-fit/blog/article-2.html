Creatine vs Pre-Workout — What You Actually Need to Know

Creatine and pre-workout are the two most commonly purchased supplements in fitness. They're often confused, often stacked, and often misunderstood. Here's a clear breakdown of what each does and when each is worth taking.

Creatine: What It Actually Is

Creatine is one of the most researched supplements in existence — over 500 peer-reviewed studies. It's a naturally occurring compound stored in muscles and used during high-intensity efforts. Supplementing with creatine monohydrate increases stored creatine by 20-40%, directly improving performance in short, explosive efforts (sprints, heavy lifts, HIIT).

What Creatine Does

  • Increases strength output (average +5-15% in compound lifts over 4-8 weeks)
  • Increases power in explosive movements
  • Speeds recovery between sets
  • May improve cognitive performance under stress

How to Take Creatine

3-5g of creatine monohydrate daily. No loading phase required — it's a myth that persists. Timing doesn't matter significantly — consistency matters. Take it every day, including rest days. It accumulates in muscle tissue over time.

Pre-Workout: What It Actually Is

Pre-workout is a category, not a compound. Most pre-workouts contain: caffeine (the main active ingredient), beta-alanine (causes the tingle, marginally improves endurance), citrulline malate (improves blood flow and reduces soreness), and various other compounds in quantities too low to matter.

The Honest Assessment

If you want the benefits of pre-workout without the proprietary blends and fillers: 200mg caffeine + 5g creatine + 6g citrulline malate is cleaner, cheaper, and more effective than most pre-workout products. The tingle from beta-alanine is harmless but provides no meaningful performance benefit at most pre-workout doses.

Who Should Take What

  • Everyone who trains: creatine monohydrate, daily
  • People who train early or tired: caffeine (coffee works fine)
  • Pre-workout products: only if you enjoy the ritual and have checked the ingredient doses are effective
