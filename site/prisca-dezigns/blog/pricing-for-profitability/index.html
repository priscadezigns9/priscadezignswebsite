Pricing for Profitability: The No-Nonsense Guide.

Let’s cut to the chase: if you’re struggling to pay your bills or feel burnt out by "low-ball" clients, your pricing isn’t just low—it’s broken. Most creatives treat pricing like a game of Pin the Tail on the Donkey, hoping whatever number they throw out sticks. That ends today.

1. Stop Trading Time for Money

Your clients aren't buying your hours; they’re buying a result. If you can solve a $100,000 problem in ten minutes, you should be paid for the $100,000 solution, not the ten minutes. Hourly rates punish efficiency. Value-based pricing rewards expertise.

2. The "Minimum Level of Engagement"

You need a floor. A number below which you simply don't open your laptop. This isn't about being arrogant; it's about business viability. Every project has administrative overhead—emails, meetings, invoicing. If the project fee is too small, that overhead eats your profit before you even start designing.

3. Context is Everything

A logo for a local bakery and a logo for a multinational corporation serve different purposes and carry different risks. Your price should reflect the scale of the impact your work has. Higher impact equals higher value. Period.

Pricing is a muscle. The more you use it—and the more you stand by your numbers—the stronger your business becomes. Stop apologizing for your rates and start owning the value you bring to the table.
