Affiliate Disclosure

We believe in full transparency. Here's exactly how affiliate links work on our sites — and why it costs you nothing extra.

The Short Version

When you click a product link on any of our sites and make a purchase on Amazon, we may earn a small commission. This costs you nothing extra. You pay the same price you'd pay going to Amazon directly.

Our recommendations are honest and independent. We curate products because we genuinely think they're great — not because of commission rates.

What Is Affiliate Marketing?

Affiliate marketing is a way for content publishers — like our curated brand sites — to earn a small fee for recommending products. It works like this:

When you click a product link on one of our sites, that link contains a unique identifier that tells Amazon (or another retailer) that you arrived from our site. If you go on to make a purchase, Amazon pays us a small percentage of the sale as a referral fee.

This commission comes from Amazon's marketing budget — not from your pocket. The price you pay on Amazon is identical whether you click our link or go to Amazon directly. There are no hidden fees, no markups, and no catches.

Affiliate marketing is how independent publishers like us are able to create free, high-quality editorial content without charging readers or running traditional display advertising.

Honest & Independent Recommendations

We want you to trust our recommendations — and that means being honest about how we operate. Here's our editorial commitment to you:

  • We select products based on quality, relevance, and value — not commission rates.
  • We do not accept payment from brands or manufacturers to feature their products.
  • We do not inflate reviews or hide negative aspects of products we feature.
  • Commission rates on Amazon vary by product category but have no influence on which products we choose to recommend.
