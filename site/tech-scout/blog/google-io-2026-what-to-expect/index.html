Every Confirmed Announcement So Far

Google has been unusually transparent this cycle — here's everything confirmed before the keynote even starts:

🧠

Gemini Intelligence

An agentic AI layer built directly into Android. It doesn't just answer questions — it takes actions across apps, books appointments, drafts messages, and makes decisions on your behalf.

👓

Android XR Smart Glasses

Google's answer to Meta Ray-Ban. Powered by Gemini 2.5 Pro, these glasses see what you see and respond in real time. Multi-brand hardware ecosystem confirmed — not just a Google-made device.

💻

Googlebooks

Android laptops replacing Chromebooks entirely. Runs Android apps natively, built for AI-first workflows. This is the biggest shift in Google's laptop strategy since Chrome OS launched in 2011.

📱

Android 17

3D emoji (Noto 3D), enhanced Find Hub with biometric security, iOS-to-Android wireless transfer tool, Pause Point screen-time tool, and new OS verification features.

🌐

Gemini in Chrome

AI-powered browsing, smart autofill, contextual page summaries, and custom widget creation — all from within your browser. Chrome becomes a Gemini interface.

🎬

Veo Video Generation

Google's Veo video AI is expected to get a major upgrade announcement. Cinematic AI video generation, directly integrated into Google's creative suite.

Why This Google I/O Is Different

The shift from OS to intelligence layer. Google isn't updating Android — it's replacing what Android means. Gemini Intelligence turns your phone from a tool you use into an agent that acts. That's not a feature drop. That's a platform redefinition.

Three things stand out about I/O 2026 specifically:

  • Hardware is back. Google had stepped back from hardware ambition after early Pixel struggles. Android XR glasses and Googlebooks signal a return to building the full stack — device, OS, and AI.
  • The Chromebook era is ending. Googlebooks replacing Chrome OS is a quiet earthquake. Every school district, every enterprise IT team, every developer who standardised on Chromebooks is now evaluating their options.
  • Meta has competition. The Android XR glasses go directly at Meta's Ray-Ban headstart. Google's bet is that Gemini's contextual intelligence beats Meta's social graph. That fight is going to be fascinating to watch.
