Smart Home Starter Kit — What to Buy First

Most people start their smart home randomly — a voice assistant here, a smart bulb there — and end up with a collection of incompatible devices that barely work together. Building a smart home correctly starts with the hub and works outward.

Step 1: Choose Your Ecosystem

The three dominant ecosystems are Amazon Alexa, Google Home, and Apple HomeKit. Choose based on the devices you already use: iPhone users benefit most from HomeKit. Android users have the best Google Home experience. Alexa has the widest third-party device compatibility. Don't mix ecosystems — it creates the fragmentation problem you're trying to avoid.

Step 2: The Hub Device

A voice assistant speaker (Echo, Nest, or HomePod mini) is the control centre of your smart home. Place it in the room you use most. This is your remote control, timer, information source, and home automation trigger — all in one device.

Step 3: Smart Plugs

Smart plugs are the fastest, cheapest way to make existing appliances smart. Lamps, fans, coffee makers, and any device that doesn't need to be "on" constantly can be scheduled, voice-controlled, or automated through smart plugs. A pack of four costs $25-40 and immediately makes 4 devices part of your smart home.

Step 4: Smart Lighting

Smart bulbs offer scheduling, dimming, and colour control. Start in the living room and bedroom — the rooms where lighting mood matters most. Buy bulbs within your chosen ecosystem for seamless control.

Step 5: Security

A video doorbell and one outdoor camera add genuine security value. They integrate with your ecosystem for motion alerts, two-way audio, and package monitoring.

What Comes Later

Smart thermostat, smart lock, robot vacuum — all genuinely useful but higher cost. Add them once the foundation works reliably. A working simple smart home is better than an ambitious broken one.
