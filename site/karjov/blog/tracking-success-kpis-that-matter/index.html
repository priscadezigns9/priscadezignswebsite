Tracking Success: The KPIs That Actually Matter for Your Business

In the digital age, we have access to more data than ever before. You can track clicks, likes, impressions, and open rates until you're buried in charts. But for most business owners, this high volume of data creates more noise than clarity. To run a successful team and a profitable project, you have to cut through the fluff and focus on the Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) that actually drive results.

High-fidelity business management is about knowing exactly where you stand at any given moment.

1. Project Profitability

Revenue is a vanity metric; profit is the reality. Tracking exactly how many hours your team spends on a project versus the total project fee is the only way to know if you're actually growing or just staying busy. Karjov automates this by linking your team's task logs directly to your project budgets.

2. Team Capacity & Velocity

Is your team overworked, or are they underutilized? Measuring velocity—how many story points or tasks your team completes in a week—allows you to forecast delivery dates with precision. When you know your team's capacity, you can say "yes" to the right projects and "no" to burnout.

3. Client Lead Response Time

Speed is a competitive advantage. The time it takes for your team to respond to a new inquiry or a revision request is a direct signal of your agency's professionalism. Reducing friction in communication keeps client trust high and churn rates low.
