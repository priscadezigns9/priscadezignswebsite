Brand Guide

10 Quiet Luxury Brands
Worth Every Penny

Published May 14, 2026

The quiet luxury label gets applied generously — to brands that genuinely deserve it, and to those simply chasing the aesthetic. This guide focuses on the former: ten brands that consistently deliver the quality, restraint, and longevity that define the category. These are not necessarily the most expensive options, but they are the ones where the investment makes sense.

No. 01
The Row
Investment Tier · Mary-Kate & Ashley Olsen

Founded in 2006, The Row is the benchmark against which all quiet luxury is measured. Every piece is conceived around the question: what is the most perfect version of this garment? The answer is usually something in cashmere, in a precise neutral, with no unnecessary detail. The Row is not aspirational in the conventional fashion sense — it is the terminal destination. The price is high; the quality justifies it for those who can reach it.

No. 02
Toteme
Premium Contemporary · Stockholm

Swedish brand Toteme has become the quiet luxury entry point for many — and with good reason. A tightly edited range of pieces in neutral palettes, quality fabrics, and clean silhouettes, updated each season but never chasing trends. The signature scarf blazer and tailored trousers are modern classics. Toteme's price point is accessible relative to luxury houses but commands quality that sits well above fast fashion. Worth every penny of the premium.

No. 03
Lemaire
Conceptual Luxury · Paris

Christophe Lemaire's brand produces some of the most considered clothing available at any price point. The silhouettes are fluid, natural, and built around the body rather than imposed upon it. Colour palettes are earthy and nuanced. The cut is everything — Lemaire's trousers, in particular, are among the finest available in contemporary fashion. A brand for those who have moved entirely beyond status dressing.

No. 04
COS
Accessible Premium · H&M Group

COS is the quiet luxury brand for people who aren't ready to spend four figures on a single garment — and there's no shame in that. The H&M-owned Swedish label consistently produces clean, architectural pieces in quality fabrics at a fraction of designer prices. Coats, knitwear, and tailoring are COS's strongest categories. Not every piece hits, but the hits are genuine and the failures are affordable. An essential entry point for building a neutral wardrobe.

No. 05
Arket
Accessible Premium · Stockholm

Arket presents itself as a Nordic market for quality clothing — and delivers more consistently than COS, at a slightly higher price point. The fabric quality is notably good for the price range; the linen, wool, and cotton pieces in particular often rival brands charging twice as much. Arket also includes a small home section that supports the full biophilic aesthetic. For building a capsule wardrobe at an accessible price, Arket is the most consistent option available.

No. 06
Loro Piana
Ultra-Luxury Fabrics · LVMH

If The Row is the benchmark for design, Loro Piana is the benchmark for material. The Italian brand produces the finest cashmere, vicuña, and baby cashmere available — and makes garments that are, in effect, the fabric plus minimal interruption. A Loro Piana sweater is not an aesthetic statement; it is a tactile one. You wear it for how it feels, not how it looks. For those who have arrived at the point where fabric is the entirety of the conversation.

No. 07
Brunello Cucinelli
Ultra-Luxury Lifestyle · Umbria, Italy

The philosopher king of quiet luxury. Brunello Cucinelli produces extraordinarily expensive clothing rooted in the artisanal traditions of Umbria, with a brand identity built on humanistic values and craft. The pricing is sometimes inexplicable; the quality is always excellent. Cucinelli's knitwear and casualwear — particularly the suede loafers and cashmere joggers — represent the apotheosis of dressed-down luxury.

No. 08
Acne Studios
Swedish Contemporary · Stockholm

Often categorised as avant-garde, Acne Studios produces a significant proportion of genuinely wearable, quiet pieces — particularly in denim, knitwear, and coats. The quality is consistently high. The brand is broader in aesthetic than the pure quiet luxury canon but the quiet side of the collection — the tailored wool coats, the understated knitwear — rivals brands at twice the price.

No. 09
Massimo Dutti
Accessible Premium · Inditex

Inditex's quiet luxury answer to Zara, Massimo Dutti is consistently underrated. The tailoring is genuinely well-constructed, the fabric selection is better than its price point suggests, and the aesthetic is squarely in neutral, understated European professional dressing. For building a wardrobe foundation of workwear and smart-casual pieces without luxury expenditure, Massimo Dutti is the most consistent option in its price band.

No. 10
Margaret Howell
British Investment Dressing · London

Margaret Howell is one of Britain's longest-running quiet luxury propositions — understated, rigorously well-made British clothing that has not substantially changed in philosophy since the 1970s. Workwear-inspired silhouettes in excellent British woolens, linen, and cotton. The pieces are expensive by accessible standards and worth it by any long-term measure. Howell's coats and knitwear in particular have aged better than almost anything in contemporary fashion.
