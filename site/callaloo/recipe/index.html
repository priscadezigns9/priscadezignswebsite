Pelau

AM
Aunty Merle
Chaguanas, Trinidad · 23 recipes
★ 4.9
Prep
20 min
Cook
55 min
Serves
6
Difficulty
Medium

🛒 Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken pieces
  • 1½ cups parboiled rice
  • 1 can pigeon peas, drained
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar (for browning)
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 whole scotch bonnet pepper
  • 2 stalks chive / scallion
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 tsp green seasoning
  • Salt & pepper to taste
🛍️ Where to Buy
Find these key ingredients at local stores near you in Trinidad & Tobago
🫘 Pigeon Peas
Hi-Lo, Massy, Pennywise
🥥 Coconut Milk
Any supermarket · Indian groceries
🌶️ Scotch Bonnet
Local market · Produce stalls
🌿 Green Seasoning
Make your own · Hott Sauce brand at Massy

📋 Instructions

  1. 1

    Season the chicken: Wash and pat dry chicken pieces. Season with green seasoning, salt, pepper, minced garlic, and sliced scallion. Let marinate for at least 30 minutes (overnight is better).

  2. 2

    Brown the sugar: Heat a heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat. Add the 2 tbsp of brown sugar and let it melt and caramelise to a deep amber colour — this is the key to pelau's signature flavour.

  3. 3

    Brown the chicken: Add the seasoned chicken to the caramelised sugar and stir quickly to coat every piece. Cook for 5–8 minutes, turning often, until well-browned on all sides.

  4. 4

    Add the liquids: Pour in the coconut milk and chicken broth. Add the drained pigeon peas. Place the whole scotch bonnet pepper on top (do not pierce it unless you want it very hot). Bring to a boil.

  5. 5

    Cook the rice: Add the parboiled rice, stir once, then reduce heat to low. Cover and cook for 25–30 minutes until rice is tender and liquid is absorbed. Resist the urge to lift the lid too often!

  6. 6

    Rest and serve: Remove from heat and let sit covered for 5 minutes. Remove the scotch bonnet pepper. Fluff gently with a fork and serve with coleslaw, plantain, or just on its own. Enjoy!
