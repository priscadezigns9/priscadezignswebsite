Gaming Gear FAQ

The questions every gamer asks. The answers only gear obsessives can give.

For FPS games, you want a lightweight mouse (under 70g) with a high-precision optical sensor — 25,600 DPI minimum. Look for ultra-low click latency (under 1ms), a comfortable ergonomic shape for your grip style (claw, palm, or fingertip), and a polling rate of 1000Hz or higher. Top picks from Razer, Logitech G, and SteelSeries dominate the pro scene for a reason. Avoid heavy mice with lots of RGB — weight is the enemy in FPS.
Yes — if you're serious about gaming, 144Hz is the minimum. The jump from 60Hz to 144Hz is immediately noticeable: smoother motion, faster reaction time, and significantly reduced motion blur. For competitive play (FPS, battle royale), 240Hz is the current sweet spot. Pair it with a 1ms response time and G-Sync/FreeSync for tear-free gameplay. Don't let a bottleneck monitor kill your reflexes.
You can build a solid gaming setup for under $500. Start with a 144Hz 1080p monitor, a reliable mechanical keyboard (Redragon or Keychron), a mid-tier gaming mouse (Logitech G305 or SteelSeries Rival 3), and a budget gaming headset with a decent mic. Skip the RGB for now — performance first. Our AI Setup Recommender can generate a complete list matched to your exact budget. Check the "Build Your Perfect Setup" section above.
Absolutely. Mechanical keyboards offer faster actuation, greater tactile feedback, and significantly longer lifespan than membrane keyboards. For gaming, linear switches (like Cherry MX Red or Speed Silver) are preferred — they register keystrokes faster and smoother. Tactile switches (MX Brown) are great for mixed use. The key difference you'll feel immediately: anti-ghosting and N-key rollover mean every key press registers, even in intense multi-key gaming moments.
Most pro esports players use either the HyperX Cloud series, SteelSeries Arctis Pro, or Beyerdynamic DT 990 paired with a separate DAC/AMP. The pros prioritize mic clarity (for team comms), accurate audio positioning (to hear enemy footsteps), and comfort for long sessions. 7.1 surround sound is a massive competitive advantage in FPS titles. Don't overlook wired options — zero latency matters at the pro level.
Reducing input lag is a multi-step process: (1) Use a monitor with a 1ms response time and disable unnecessary post-processing effects. (2) Enable your monitor's "Game Mode" to bypass extra processing. (3) Use a wired mouse and keyboard — wireless adds 1–8ms latency. (4) Set your in-game resolution to match native monitor resolution. (5) Raise your polling rate to 1000Hz on your mouse. (6) Keep GPU drivers updated and cap your frame rate just below your monitor's refresh rate using RTSS. These steps combined can reduce total system latency by 30–50ms.

Frequently Asked Questions

What gaming mouse is best for FPS games?

For FPS, prioritize low weight (<80g), high polling rate (1000Hz+), and a reliable optical sensor. Top picks: Razer DeathAdder V2, Logitech G Pro X Superlight 2, Zowie EC2-C.

Do I need a 144Hz gaming monitor?

If you play competitive FPS (CS2, Valorant, Apex), 144Hz makes a real, measurable difference in reaction time. For RPG or strategy games, 60Hz is fine.

What is the best budget gaming setup?

Under $500: Logitech G305 mouse, Redragon K552 keyboard, AOC C24G1 monitor, HyperX Cloud Stinger headset. Solid 1080p competitive rig.

Is a mechanical keyboard worth it for gaming?

Yes — tactile feedback, faster actuation, and durability (50M+ keystroke rating) vs membrane keyboards. Linear switches (Red) are fastest for gaming.

What headset do pro gamers use?

Most pros use SteelSeries Arctis Nova Pro, Beyerdynamic DT 990 Pro (with DAC/amp), or HyperX Cloud Alpha. Surround sound matters less than mic quality.

How do I reduce input lag?

Use a wired mouse and keyboard, enable 'Game Mode' on your monitor, cap FPS at your monitor's refresh rate, and close background applications.
