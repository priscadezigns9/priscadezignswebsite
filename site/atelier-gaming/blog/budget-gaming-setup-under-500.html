How to Build Your First Gaming Setup

Building a gaming setup from scratch is overwhelming if you approach it without a framework. Start with what matters most, spend where it counts, and build outward.

Priority Order

Most people buy peripherals first and the chair last. This is backwards. The chair is what you sit in for hours — a bad chair causes real physical damage over time. The correct order: chair → desk → monitor → peripherals → aesthetics.

The Chair

An ergonomic office chair often outperforms gaming chairs at the same price point. Look for: lumbar support (adjustable, not just a pillow), armrest height and angle adjustment, seat depth adjustment, breathable mesh or quality foam. Budget minimum: $150 for something that won't hurt you. $300-500 gets you into genuinely good territory.

The Desk

Surface size matters more than aesthetics. Minimum 120cm wide for a comfortable single-monitor setup. 150cm+ for dual monitors. Depth of 60-70cm gives room for your keyboard and enough distance from your monitor. Solid wood or steel-frame desks at any price point beat particleboard.

The Monitor

For competitive gaming: 1080p at 144Hz minimum, 1ms response time. For immersive/single-player: 1440p at 144Hz is the sweet spot in 2026. 4K at 60Hz is still enjoyable but not ideal for fast-paced games.

Peripherals

Mouse first (most impactful for aim), then keyboard, then headset. A lightweight mouse (under 80g) with a reliable optical sensor is the foundation of good aim. Headset: stereo is fine for most games — 7.1 surround is often software-processed and can reduce clarity.

Lighting and Aesthetics

LED strips behind the monitor (bias lighting) reduce eye strain and add atmosphere. This is the last thing to spend on — but one of the most noticeable upgrades once everything else is in place.
