Grow With Confidence

The four most popular indoor plants — and exactly how to keep them alive and thriving. No guesswork.

🌿
Pothos
WateringEvery 1–2 weeks. Let top inch of soil dry out first.
LightLow to bright indirect light. Tolerates shade well.
HumidityAverage household humidity is fine (40–60%).
Easy — Beginner Friendly
🌱
Monstera
WateringEvery 1–2 weeks. Allow soil to partially dry between waterings.
LightBright, indirect light. Avoid harsh direct sun.
HumidityLoves humidity — mist occasionally or use a pebble tray.
Easy – Low Maintenance
🪴
Snake Plant
WateringEvery 2–6 weeks. Highly drought-tolerant — less is more.
LightAlmost any light level, including very low light.
HumidityThrives in dry air. Ideal for heated or air-conditioned rooms.
Easy – Nearly Indestructible
🌸
Peace Lily
WateringEvery 1–1.5 weeks. It will droop slightly when thirsty — your signal.
LightLow to medium indirect light. Avoid direct sun entirely.
HumidityPrefers higher humidity. Mist regularly or place near a humidifier.
Medium – Worth the Care

Biophilic Design

Why Plants Change Everything

Science increasingly confirms what our instincts already know: living around plants makes us feel better. Here's what the research actually says.

🧠
Reduce Stress & Anxiety

Studies show that simply being around plants lowers cortisol levels. A desk plant can reduce psychological and physiological stress during tasks by up to 37%.

💨
Improve Air Quality

Certain plants absorb VOCs and pollutants from the air. Snake plants and peace lilies are among NASA's top-recommended air-purifying species.

🏡
Transform Your Space

Biophilic design — incorporating natural elements into interiors — has been shown to increase creativity, focus, and overall wellbeing in both homes and workplaces.

🌿
Build a Living Habit

Caring for plants instils routine and mindfulness. Tending to something living creates a gentle daily ritual that grounds you in the present moment.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the easiest plants for beginners?

Pothos, Snake Plant (Sansevieria), ZZ Plant, and Spider Plant. All tolerate low light, irregular watering, and are nearly impossible to kill — perfect starting points.

How often should I water my succulents?

Every 2–3 weeks in summer, monthly in winter. The rule: water deeply, then let the soil dry completely before watering again. Root rot from overwatering kills more succulents than drought.

What eco-friendly swaps make the biggest difference?

Reusable bags and bottles (eliminates 700+ plastics/year), compostable kitchen bags, bamboo toothbrushes, and concentrated cleaning tablets. These four changes cut household plastic by ~60%.

How do I know if my plant needs more light?

Signs of insufficient light: leggy growth (stretching toward windows), pale or yellowing leaves, slow or no growth, and soil staying wet too long. Move 1–2 feet closer to your light source.

What is composting and how do I start?

Composting turns kitchen and garden waste into nutrient-rich soil amendment. Start with a countertop bin for fruit/vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, and eggshells. Avoid meat, dairy, and oils.

What plants improve indoor air quality?

Peace Lily, Boston Fern, English Ivy, and Areca Palm remove common VOCs (formaldehyde, benzene, xylene). NASA research supports 1 medium plant per 100 sq ft for measurable air improvement.
