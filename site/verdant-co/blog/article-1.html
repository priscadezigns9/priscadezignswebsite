10 Easy Eco Swaps for Your Home

Sustainable living does not require a complete lifestyle overhaul. The most effective changes are the ones you can make once and maintain without effort. These ten swaps are exactly that: set them up and they run themselves.

1. Reusable Grocery Bags

Keep them in your car or bag so they are always available. The barrier to using them is proximity — if they require a trip to the cupboard, you will forget them at the door. Fold them flat and keep them where the decision gets made.

2. Beeswax Wraps Instead of Cling Film

Washable, reusable, and effective for covering bowls, wrapping cheese, and keeping cut produce fresh. One set lasts a year or more and eliminates hundreds of metres of single-use plastic.

3. Bamboo Toothbrushes

Functionally identical to plastic toothbrushes. Compostable handle (remove the nylon bristles first). Simple swap with no behaviour change required.

4. Refillable Soap and Shampoo

Switch to concentrated refill pouches or bars. A shampoo bar lasts 2-3 times longer than a plastic bottle of liquid shampoo. Castile soap concentrate replaces multiple cleaning products in one.

5. LED Bulbs Throughout

If you haven't replaced all bulbs already, do it now. LED bulbs use 75% less energy than incandescent, last 15-25 times longer, and pay back their cost within months.

6. Cloth Napkins

A set of linen or cotton cloth napkins washed weekly replaces thousands of paper napkins over their lifetime. They also make every meal feel more considered.

7. Reusable Water Bottle and Coffee Cup

Thermal insulated stainless steel keeps drinks cold for 24 hours and hot for 12. This is the highest-frequency swap on the list — most people buy a coffee or water several times a week.

8. Compostable Bin Liners

Or no bin liner at all in dry waste bins. Most bin liners are unnecessary for dry waste — just empty and wipe. Use compostable liners only where genuinely needed.

9. Solid Dish Soap

A solid dish soap bar lasts longer than liquid, comes without plastic packaging, and cleans just as effectively. Pair with a natural bristle dish brush (no plastic handle) for a completely plastic-free washing-up setup.

10. Smart Power Strips

Electronics on standby consume 5-10% of home energy use. A smart power strip cuts power to devices in standby automatically, or lets you switch them all off at once. Set it up once, save continuously.
