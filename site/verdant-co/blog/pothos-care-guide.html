15 Best Indoor Plants for 2026

By Verdant Co Editors · Published May 16, 2026

The indoor plant market has never been broader or more accessible. But with thousands of options available, choosing which plants are actually worth your time, space, and care can be overwhelming. We've ranked our 15 favourite indoor plants for 2026 based on three criteria: ease of care, visual impact, and real-world growing experience. Whether you're a first-time plant parent or an experienced grower, there's something on this list for you.

The List

No. 01
Pothos (Epipremnum aureum)
BeginnerLow LightTrailing

The quintessential starter plant. Tolerates low light, irregular watering, and most temperature ranges. Grows fast, trails beautifully, and propagates with minimal effort. The golden pothos with its yellow-green variegation is the classic; neon and marble queen varieties offer more visual drama.

No. 02
Snake Plant (Sansevieria trifasciata)
BeginnerAny LightAir Purifying

Architectural, upright, and nearly indestructible. Snake plants survive drought conditions that would kill most plants, tolerate low light, and are one of NASA's top-recommended air-purifying species. The 'Laurentii' variety with yellow-edged leaves is the most popular; 'Moonshine' with pale silver-green leaves is increasingly sought after.

No. 03
Monstera deliciosa
IntermediateBright IndirectStatement

The split-leaf philodendron that defines tropical interior design. Large fenestrated leaves that develop their characteristic holes as the plant matures. Grows to impressive size with time and good light. Relatively forgiving once established, though it needs more consistent care than the top two picks.

No. 04
ZZ Plant (Zamioculcas zamiifolia)
BeginnerLow LightDrought Tolerant

Glossy, dark green leaves on arching stems. Stores water in underground rhizomes, making it extraordinarily drought tolerant. One of the few plants that genuinely thrives in low-light office environments. The 'Raven' variety with nearly black foliage is one of the most dramatic indoor plants available.

No. 05
Peace Lily (Spathiphyllum)
BeginnerLow LightFlowering

One of the few flowering plants that genuinely tolerates low light. White flowers appear seasonally, the glossy dark green leaves are attractive year-round, and it's one of the most effective air-purifying houseplants available. It wilts dramatically when thirsty — a useful watering indicator.

No. 06
Rubber Plant (Ficus elastica)
EasyBright IndirectStatement

Large, waxy leaves in deep burgundy, dark green, or variegated cream and green. Grows into a small tree over time, making it a dramatic statement plant for living rooms. The 'Burgundy' variety with near-black leaves is particularly striking against pale walls.

No. 07
Heartleaf Philodendron (Philodendron hederaceum)
BeginnerLow-Medium LightTrailing

Heart-shaped glossy leaves on long trailing stems. Similar in habit to pothos but with a more fluid, graceful trailing form. Tolerates low light, grows fast, and is highly forgiving. The 'Lemon Lime' variety with bright chartreuse foliage is particularly vibrant.

No. 08
Bird of Paradise (Strelitzia reginae)
IntermediateBright LightLarge Statement

Paddle-shaped leaves on tall stems create one of the most architecturally dramatic indoor plants available. Needs significant bright light — ideally a south or west-facing window — but rewards with extraordinary presence. Grows slowly but steadily to ceiling height over time.

No. 09
Calathea / Maranta
IntermediateIndirect LightPatterned Foliage

Extraordinary patterned leaves in combinations of green, cream, burgundy, and purple. Some varieties fold their leaves upward at night (prayer plant behaviour). More demanding in terms of humidity and water quality — best in rooms with consistent warmth and away from direct sun.

No. 10
String of Pearls (Senecio rowleyanus)
IntermediateBright IndirectTrailing

Spherical bead-like leaves on trailing stems. Visually unique — no other plant looks quite like it. Ideal in hanging baskets or on high shelves where it can trail downward. Succulent-like watering needs: allow to dry thoroughly between waterings.

No. 11
Fiddle Leaf Fig (Ficus lyrata)
IntermediateBright IndirectStatement Tree

Large, violin-shaped leaves on a slender trunk. Favoured by architects and interior designers for its clean, graphic presence. Temperamental — hates drafts, sudden temperature changes, and inconsistent watering — but spectacular when happy in a permanent, well-lit position.

No. 12
Boston Fern (Nephrolepis exaltata)
IntermediateIndirect LightHumidity Lover

Lush, arching fronds create a soft, feathery texture. One of the most effective air-humidifying plants indoors — it releases moisture vapour as a byproduct of transpiration. Best in humid rooms; ideal for bathrooms with natural light.

No. 13
Aloe Vera
BeginnerBright DirectPractical

Succulent rosette with thick, fleshy leaves. Thrives on neglect and loves direct sunlight — ideal for kitchen windowsills. The gel inside the leaves has genuine skincare and first-aid applications. Water infrequently and never let it sit in standing water.

No. 14
Monstera adansonii (Swiss Cheese Vine)
EasyIndirect LightTrailing/Climbing

Smaller-leaved cousin of Monstera deliciosa with heavily perforated leaves. Trails or climbs enthusiastically. More manageable in size than its famous relative but equally striking. Often easier to find and less expensive.

No. 15
Alocasia 'Polly' (African Mask Plant)
IntermediateBright IndirectArchitectural

Arrow-shaped leaves with striking white veining against deep green. Bold and architectural. Needs consistent warmth, humidity, and good light — more demanding than most on this list — but the visual payoff is substantial. Goes dormant in winter; don't panic if it loses leaves in colder months.
