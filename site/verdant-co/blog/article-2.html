The Best Reusable Products for a Zero-Waste Kitchen

The kitchen generates more household waste than any other room. Most of it is packaging, single-use plastic, and disposable products. These reusable alternatives address the highest-volume waste streams first.

Silicone Food Storage Bags

Replaces zip-lock bags for food storage, freezing, and marinating. Dishwasher-safe, freezer-safe, and oven-safe to 200C. A set of four replaces hundreds of disposable bags over their lifetime. Look for platinum-grade silicone (food-safe and durable).

Glass Food Containers

Glass containers with locking lids are airtight, microwave-safe, oven-safe, and don't stain or absorb odours. They outlast plastic by decades and don't leach chemicals into food. The upfront cost is higher; the lifetime cost is lower.

Beeswax Wraps

A wax-coated cotton wrap that clings to surfaces using hand warmth. Effective for covering bowls, wrapping produce, and storing cheese. Wash in cool water and air dry. Lasts 6-12 months with regular use.

Compost Bin

Food scraps make up 30-40% of household waste by weight. A countertop compost bin with a lid captures daily food waste for composting. If you don't have a garden, many areas have composting collection services or community drop-off points.

Reusable Produce Bags

Lightweight mesh or organic cotton bags replace plastic produce bags at the market. They wash easily and weigh nothing, so they don't affect produce weight at checkout.

Cloth Paper Towel Alternatives

Unpaper towels (cloth squares on a roll) replace paper towels for most tasks. Use for wiping surfaces, hands, and spills. Wash with regular laundry. A roll of 12 replaces hundreds of paper towel sheets per year.

Stainless Steel Straws

Reusable straws with a cleaning brush. A simple swap for the highest single-use plastic volume item most people encounter.
