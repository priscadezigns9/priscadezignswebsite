The Architecture Rules

01
Sierra is the Only Front Door
Every customer interaction — inquiry, complaint, booking, payment — enters the business through Sierra. No other AI employee ever speaks directly to a customer. Sierra is the wall between the public and the internal operation.
02
Internal Employees Process Data — They Do Not Interact
Dean, Jamal, Alice, IT, QA — they process data about customers (leads, payments, orders) but never interact with customers directly. They talk only to their department team, or to the business owner via reports and alerts.
03
Every Employee is Standalone First
Each AI employee delivers full value on its own, without requiring another employee to be purchased. A business with only Dean gets full marketing data intelligence. When they add Sierra later, Sierra feeds Dean — compounding the value without overlap.
04
Cross-Department Employees Receive Signals — They Do Not Override
Alice (HR), IT, and QA/HSE receive signals from every department. They serve all departments but stay in their own lane. Alice handles people. IT handles systems. QA/HSE handles compliance. They connect but never take over another department's function.
05
No Overlap. Ever.
Dean does not schedule social media (that is a human marketing task). Jamal does not collect payment from customers (that is Sierra's lane). Alice does not manage customer complaints (that is Sierra's lane). Each employee has one job. One audience. One output.
06
The Full Vision
A client brings their product or service. Prisca Dezigns builds the entire operating layer around it using AI employees. Every department is covered. The owner focuses on vision and delivery. The AI employees handle every repetitive, time-consuming, high-error task in the background — autonomously, compliantly, and around the clock.
