Caribbean HSE Intelligence

The Gaps Luna Was Built to Close

We researched the Caribbean HSE landscape — from micro-businesses in Trinidad to large manufacturing operations across CARICOM. Here is what the data shows, and exactly how Luna responds to each gap.

Pain Point 01

No Dedicated HSE Officer

80%+ of Caribbean SMEs cannot afford a qualified HSE professional (TT$8K–$18K/month). A part-time admin fills the gap — and fails during audits.

Pain Point 02

JSA & Documentation Backlog

Job Safety Analyses take 3–4 hours to complete manually. Most small businesses skip them entirely — creating direct audit liability under the OSH Act.

Pain Point 03

Zero Near-Miss Culture

Near-misses go unreported in the Caribbean because there is no easy, anonymous channel. Without data, incidents escalate to injuries before anyone acts.

Pain Point 04

Training Records Never Maintained

Most businesses cannot prove their workers received safety training during an OSHA-TT inspection — a common reason for failed audits and enforcement action.

Pain Point 05

No Safety Metrics Ever Tracked

TRIR, LTIFR, and DART rates are standard compliance metrics worldwide. Almost no Caribbean SME calculates them — making performance blind and audit readiness impossible.

Pain Point 06

Corrective Actions Left Open

When a hazard is flagged, there is rarely a system to track the fix. Inspectors find the same violations year after year because no one closed the loop.
