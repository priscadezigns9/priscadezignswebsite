The 5-Step Glow Protocol Routine

The most effective skincare routines are built on a clear, repeatable framework. Glow Protocol's approach is simple: cleanse, tone, serum, moisturise, protect. Apply this sequence consistently — morning and evening — and your skin will reward you.

Step 01

Cleanse

Start with a gentle, pH-balanced cleanser to remove impurities without stripping your natural moisture barrier.

Step 02

Tone

A hydrating toner or essence prepares the skin to absorb active ingredients more effectively.

Step 03

Serum

Your targeted treatment step — this is where powerhouse ingredients do their best work.

Step 04

Moisturise

Lock in hydration and support the skin barrier with a moisturiser suited to your skin type.

Step 05

SPF

The most important step of your AM routine. Broad-spectrum SPF 50 protects against UV damage and prevents premature ageing.

The Powerhouse Ingredients of 2026

In 2026, formulations are smarter than ever — and three ingredients have risen to the top of every serious skincare routine discussion: niacinamide, retinol, and peptides. Understanding how and when to use each is the difference between good skin and transformative skin.

  • Niacinamide (Vitamin B3): The ultimate multi-tasker. Niacinamide brightens, minimises pores, reduces inflammation, and strengthens the skin barrier — safe for all skin types and appropriate for both AM and PM use. Layer it beneath your moisturiser for best results.
  • Retinol: The gold standard of anti-ageing and cell renewal. Retinol accelerates skin turnover, smooths fine lines, and keeps hyperpigmentation in check. Use it in your PM routine only — start 2-3 nights per week and build up. Always follow with SPF the next morning.
  • Peptides: Signal molecules that instruct the skin to produce more collagen and elastin. Peptides are gentle, effective, and safe to layer with most other actives. They work beautifully in serums and moisturisers, making them ideal for everyday use.

The key to ingredient success is patience and consistency. Most actives take 8-12 weeks to show full results. Trust the process.

Morning vs. Evening Routine: Key Differences

Your AM skincare routine should focus on protection and hydration: gentle cleanse, hydrating toner, antioxidant-rich serum (Vitamin C or niacinamide), moisturiser, and SPF 50. Your PM routine is for repair and renewal: double cleanse if you wore makeup or SPF, hydrating toner, retinol or peptide serum, nourishing moisturiser, and optionally a face oil or overnight mask.

Never skip SPF in the morning — not even on cloudy days, not even if you work indoors. UV rays penetrate glass and contribute to up to 90% of visible skin ageing. It is the single highest-impact step in your entire routine.
