Common Concerns

Myths vs. Facts

It is normal for organisations to have questions before transitioning to an EV fleet. Here is what the data says.

❓ "Chinese EVs are unreliable."
BYD is the world's largest EV manufacturer by volume, surpassing Tesla in global sales in 2023. GAC Aion has delivered over 1.5 million EVs worldwide. These are not experimental brands — they are the benchmark for mass-market EV reliability. JD Power and Euro NCAP data consistently rate BYD vehicles at or above equivalent Japanese and Korean competitors.
❓ "What happens if the battery fails?"
Every EVOLVE vehicle carries an 8-year manufacturer warranty on the battery pack. Battery degradation across BYD and GAC fleets averages just 2.3% per year — meaning after 8 years, the battery still holds over 81% of its original capacity. Real-world fleet data from global operators confirms EV batteries consistently outlast their warranty periods.
❓ "What if parts are not available locally?"
EVOLVE stocks genuine parts locally and holds direct import agreements with all manufacturers. Where a part is not on-island, it is ordered directly from the factory — no grey market, no third-party suppliers. SBCS University-trained technicians handle all servicing in-country.
❓ "We cannot charge vehicles at our compound."
Standard AC charging requires only a regular 240V outlet — the same type used for heavy-duty appliances. No specialised infrastructure is needed to get started. A fleet of 5 vehicles charging overnight adds approximately TT$100 to the nightly electricity bill — a fraction of daily petrol costs.
❓ "EVs do not have enough range for our operations."
The BYD Atto 3 delivers up to 420 km per charge. The GAC Aion LX Plus reaches 1,008 km on a single charge under CLTC testing. Trinidad & Tobago is approximately 80 km end to end — a full charge covers an entire week of typical corporate driving without needing a top-up.
❓ "EV maintenance is complicated and expensive."
EVs have up to 90% fewer moving parts than internal combustion vehicles. There is no oil, no transmission fluid, no spark plugs, no exhaust system. Global fleet operators report 40–60% lower maintenance costs compared to equivalent petrol fleets over a 5-year period.
