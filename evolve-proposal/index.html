Why Electric — Why Now

The global shift to electric vehicles is no longer a trend — it's an economic and operational reality. Organisations that act early lock in cost advantages that late movers cannot recover.

The Global Direction

Global EV sales surpassed 17 million units in 2024 — over 20% of all new vehicle sales worldwide. Governments across Europe, Asia, and the Americas have committed to phasing out internal combustion engines between 2030 and 2040. This is not a speculative future. It is already the present reality in most developed markets.

For Trinidad & Tobago — with fuel subsidy volatility and rising imported parts costs — locking in a low-maintenance, electrically-powered fleet is a strategic hedge that pays dividends from the first month of operation.

Common Concerns

Myths vs. Facts

It is normal for organisations to have questions before transitioning to an EV fleet. Here is what the data says.

"Chinese EVs are unreliable."
BYD is the world's largest EV manufacturer by volume, surpassing Tesla in global sales in 2023. GAC Aion has delivered over 1.5 million EVs worldwide. JD Power and Euro NCAP data consistently rate BYD, GAC, Leapmotor, and Denza at or above Japanese and Korean competitors. Leapmotor earned a 5-star Euro NCAP rating in 2023. Denza is a joint venture with Mercedes-Benz — premium engineering at Caribbean pricing.
"What happens if the battery fails?"
Every EVOLVE vehicle carries an 8-year manufacturer warranty on the battery pack. Battery degradation averages just 2.3% per year — meaning after 8 years, the battery still holds over 81% of its original capacity. Real-world fleet data from global operators confirms EV batteries consistently outlast their warranty periods.
"What if parts are not available locally?"
Genuine replacement parts are supplied directly by EVOLVE Mobility. Where a part is not currently available locally, EVOLVE coordinates sourcing on your behalf — no grey market, no unverified third-party suppliers. All servicing is carried out by SBCS University-trained technicians in-country.
"What about public charging infrastructure?"
Standard AC charging requires only a regular 240V outlet — the same type used for heavy-duty appliances. No specialised infrastructure needed to get started. A fleet of 5 vehicles charging overnight adds approximately TT$100 to the nightly electricity bill. EVOLVE operates 8 charging stations across Trinidad & Tobago, with more being added. TTT vehicles can use this network for top-ups during operating hours, while overnight compound charging remains the primary and most cost-efficient method.
"EVs don't have enough range for our operations."
The BYD Atto 3 delivers up to 420km per charge. The GAC Aion V reaches 600km. Trinidad & Tobago is approximately 80km end to end — a full charge covers an entire week of typical corporate driving without needing a top-up.
"EVs are more likely to catch fire."
The data says the opposite. Electric vehicles experience approximately 25 fires per 100,000 vehicles sold. Petrol and diesel vehicles: 1,530 per 100,000 — over 60× higher. Hybrids: 3,475 per 100,000. EVs have no fuel tank, no combustible liquid, and no hot exhaust system — significantly reducing the ignition risk that causes most roadside fires.
"Charging at a public station is a security risk."
This is actually one of EV's strongest safety advantages for TTT. Your compound charger is installed on your own premises. Vehicles charge overnight in a secure, controlled environment — no need to stop at a public gas station at any hour. Every morning, every vehicle leaves fully charged, fully safe. For a corporate fleet operating across T&T, this is a meaningful operational and personal security upgrade.
