NVIDIA VERA RUBIN

Live from Computex 2026 in Taipei, NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang has unveiled the "Vera Rubin" platform—the successor to Blackwell and the definitive architecture for the era of Physical AI.

The Vera Rubin architecture represents the most significant structural pivot in NVIDIA’s history. Named after the astronomer who provided evidence for the existence of dark matter, the platform is designed to handle the "Dark Matter of Data"—the chaotic, high-fidelity inputs required for robots and autonomous agents to perceive and interact with the physical world in real-time.

THE VERA CPU & RUBIN GPU

At the heart of the new platform is the **Vera CPU**, a high-performance Arm-based processor designed specifically for agentic orchestration. Combined with the **Rubin GPU**, which features a new "Physical Processing Unit" (PPU), NVIDIA is moving beyond simple LLM inference and toward a "Multimodal Physics Engine" that runs natively on the silicon.

RTX SPARK: NEURAL RENDERING

For the consumer segment, NVIDIA teased **RTX Spark**, a specialized desktop GPU that utilizes neural rendering to bypass traditional rasterization entirely. By generating frames through a persistent neural field, Spark promises 8K resolution at 120fps with a fraction of the power draw seen in the RTX 50-series.
