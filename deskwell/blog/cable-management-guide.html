Cable Management 101: Clean Desk Setup Tips

A cluttered cable situation doesn't just look bad — it creates cognitive noise that affects focus. Here's a systematic approach to achieving a genuinely clean desk, regardless of how many devices you're running.

Step 1: Audit and Eliminate

Before buying any cable management products, unplug everything and audit. Most setups have 2-3 phantom cables from old devices no longer in use. Remove them. Then assess what truly needs a wired connection — smart home devices, webcams, and some peripherals can go wireless, dramatically reducing cable count.

Step 2: Route, Don't Just Bundle

The amateur approach is bundling cables together and hoping for the best. The pro approach is routing — planning the path each cable takes from device to power source with minimal crossing. Cables that need to move (laptop chargers, headphone cables) stay accessible. Permanent cables (desktop power, monitors) get hidden.

The Essential Products

Cable raceways: adhesive channels that run along desk edges or walls — clean and paintable. Under-desk cable trays: mount to the desk underside to hide power strips and excess cable length. Velcro ties: reusable and infinitely adjustable, far superior to zip ties. Cable clips: adhesive clips that route individual cables along desk legs or edges. Cable sleeves: neoprene or mesh wraps that bundle multiple cables into one clean run.

The Under-Desk Power Setup

The foundation of clean cable management is a single, well-placed power strip mounted under the desk. Everything plugs into it, and only one cable drops to the wall outlet. Mount the strip with adhesive cable management clips or a dedicated under-desk mount. This single change eliminates 80% of visible cable chaos.
