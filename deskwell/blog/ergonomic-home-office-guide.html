The Complete Ergonomic Home Office Guide

Published May 15, 2026 · By the Deskwell Editorial Team

Back pain, wrist strain, eye fatigue — these aren't inevitable consequences of desk work. They're the result of poorly configured workstations. This guide covers every ergonomic adjustment you need to make, plus the products that help you make them.

A 2025 study by the British Journal of Occupational Therapy found that home workers are 42% more likely to experience musculoskeletal complaints than office workers — primarily because their workstations have never been properly configured. The office had a facilities team to set things up; at home, you're on your own.

The good news: most ergonomic improvements cost almost nothing. They're adjustments, not purchases. But a few well-chosen pieces of gear can make a profound difference to how you feel at the end of an 8-hour day.

The 7 Rules of Ergonomic Setup

01
Monitor at Eye Level

The top of your monitor should be at or just below eye level. Looking down or up for hours causes neck strain that accumulates over months. A monitor arm or a simple riser solves this for $25–160.

02
Arms at 90 Degrees

When typing, your elbows should be at roughly 90 degrees and your forearms parallel to the floor. If your desk is too high, an adjustable chair fixes this. If the chair won't go high enough, a footrest brings your feet level.

03
Lumbar Support is Non-Negotiable

Your lower back (lumbar spine) needs support when seated. If your chair doesn't have it, a $25 lumbar pillow will make an immediate difference. If you're spending 6+ hours at a desk daily, invest in a proper ergonomic chair.

04
Monitor Distance: Arm's Length

Sit back in your chair and extend your arm. Your fingertips should just reach (or almost reach) the screen. Too close causes eye strain; too far causes you to lean forward, destroying your posture.

05
Wrist Neutral, Not Bent

Your wrists should float level with your keyboard — not bent up or down. A thin wrist rest (especially for the mouse) reduces the micro-strain that accumulates into RSI over months.

06
Feet Flat on the Floor

Or on a footrest. Dangling feet cut off circulation and cause hip discomfort. If your chair's lowest setting still leaves your feet dangling, a footrest ($20–35) solves this completely.

07
Stand Up Every 60 Minutes

No desk setup compensates for prolonged static sitting. Set a timer. Stand, walk, stretch — even for 2 minutes. A standing desk makes this friction-free, but a timer on your phone achieves the same health outcome for free.
