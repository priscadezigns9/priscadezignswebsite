THE DESKWELL METHOD

Build Your Perfect Workspace

Whether you're setting up a home office, upgrading your school desk, or outfitting a team — here's the order of operations.

01

Start With Your Chair

Your chair is the most important purchase. Spend here first. An ergonomic chair prevents the back pain that kills productivity. Look for: adjustable lumbar, seat depth, armrests. Budget at least $200.

02

Get Your Monitor Right

Eye level, arm's length away. One or two monitors changes everything. Add a monitor arm — it frees your desk and lets you adjust height/tilt properly. Your neck will thank you.

03

Manage Your Cables

Cable chaos is a focus killer. Before you buy anything else, route your cables. A cable management tray ($25) and velcro ties ($8) transform a messy desk into a professional setup.

04

Light It Properly

Bad lighting causes eye strain and ruins video calls. Natural light to the side (not behind you). Add a desk lamp with warm/cool temperature control. Bias lighting behind your monitor reduces eye fatigue.

05

Add Your Finishing Touches

Desk mat, plant, cable box, small whiteboard. These are not luxuries — they define how you feel at your desk every day. A desk you love is a desk you work well at.

WORK SMARTER

The 5 Rules of a Productive Workspace

Research-backed. Jargon-free. Applicable today.

The 20-20-20 Rule

Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds. Prevents eye strain. Set a timer.

Desk Height Formula

Sitting: elbows at 90°, forearms parallel to desk. Standing: elbows at 90°, slightly higher than sitting. Most people's desks are too high.

The Two-Monitor Rule

Primary monitor directly in front. Secondary at a slight angle. Never turn your head more than 30° to see it.

Temperature Matters

Studies show 70–77°F (21–25°C) is optimal for office productivity. Too cold = distraction. Too warm = drowsiness.

Plant = Focus

Studies by the University of Exeter found that plants in the workspace increase productivity by 15%. One plant. That's it.
