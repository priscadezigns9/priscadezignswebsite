Phillips Geneva Auction XXIII: The New Hierarchy

May 26, 2026 • Geneva, Switzerland

The hammer has fallen. Phillips, in association with Bacs & Russo, has once again redefined the ceiling of the watch market.

Auction XXIII was not just about the million-dollar lots; it was an insight into a new hierarchy. As the room at La Réserve erupted in applause, three distinct trends emerged that will define the rest of 2026.

1. Independent Dominance

  • F.P. Journe 'Souscription' Tourbillon Realized: $4,240,000. Collectors are prioritizing early, hand-finished masterpieces over mass-produced luxury. The gap between "industrial" and "artisanal" has never been wider.
  • Akrivia AK-06 Steel Realized: $680,000. Rexhep Rexhepi's work continues to defy gravity, establishing a new floor for modern independent horology.

2. Neo-Vintage Momentum

Patek Philippe 3970s and 3940s in platinum saw a 20% uptick in realization prices compared to last season. We are seeing a flight to quality, wearability, and "quiet luxury" in the most literal sense. The market is moving away from oversized pieces back to the classic proportions of the 90s.

3. The Steel Sport Cool-off

While the Nautilus 5711 and Royal Oak 15202 remain staples of any serious collection, the "hype" premiums are stabilizing. Realized prices for standard iterations have flattened, making room for more refined complications—perpetual calendars and split-second chronographs are the new targets for smart capital.
