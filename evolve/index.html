MYTH VS. REALITY

The Myth

"Electric Vehicles are unsafe in heavy Caribbean rain and floods."

The Reality

Our entire EVolve fleet features IP68-rated sealed battery systems. They are tested to operate safely in up to 2 feet of water for 30 minutes, ensuring your business keeps moving even in the toughest tropical conditions.

The Myth

"Charging takes too long and disrupts my business fleet schedule."

The Reality

With public DC Fast Charging, you can reach 80% charge in 30-45 minutes—the exact time for a lunch break or courier handover. For home/office fleets, overnight charging is as simple as plugging in a phone.

The Myth

"Electric car batteries only last a few years before needing replacement."

The Reality

Modern LFP batteries are designed for 3,000+ charge cycles, which translates to 15-20 years of typical use. Prisca Dezigns backs this with a comprehensive 8-year dedicated battery warranty.

The Myth

"Electric cars lack the power needed for heavy hills and highways."

The Reality

Electric motors provide instant 100% torque from 0 RPM. Whether climbing Lady Young Road or merging onto the highway, the acceleration is superior to gasoline counterparts, as seen in the Hyptec HT's 5.8s launch.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

How do I charge my EVolve vehicle?

You can charge at home using a standard 220V outlet (Wallbox) which we install. A full charge costs approximately $20.00 TT at the current rate of 40¢ per kilowatt.

What is the warranty coverage?

We provide a comprehensive 5-year vehicle warranty and a dedicated 8-year warranty on the battery pack.

Can I test drive these vehicles?

Yes, we offer demo drives for qualified buyers. Use the "Book Demo Drive" button to schedule your session with Priscilla Narine.

What about flooding and water?

Our vehicles carry high IP ratings. The iCaur 03, for example, is IP68 rated, meaning it is tested for up to 2 feet of water for 30 minutes. While flooding is never recommended, our EVs offer superior protection against water ingress compared to traditional engines.

How long does a full charge take?

Charging time varies. A standard home wallbox can charge most vehicles overnight (6-8 hours). Public fast chargers can reach 80% charge in approximately 30-45 minutes.

Are there charging stations in Trinidad?

Yes, the public charging network is expanding rapidly across both islands, with fast chargers at major malls and service stations.

What are the maintenance costs?

EVs have approximately 90% fewer moving parts than gas vehicles. There are no oil changes, spark plugs, or transmission flushes required. Maintenance is largely focused on tires, brakes, and cabin filters.
