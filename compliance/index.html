03 — Enforcement Architecture

Compliance is not a feature. It is the foundation.

Three layers ensure every agent stays within legal bounds — at the prompt level, the knowledge level, and the escalation level.

📝

Layer 1 — System Prompt

Every agent carries a jurisdiction-aware system prompt that defines what laws apply, what the agent can state, and what must be escalated to a human.

  • Territory declared at deployment
  • Legal scope hard-coded per agent role
  • Prohibits fabricating legal interpretations
  • Escalation triggers built in
📚

Layer 2 — Knowledge Base

A compliance reference document is attached to each agent at runtime. The agent can only cite what is in the document — it never guesses.

  • Caribbean Labour Law Reference
  • Jurisdiction-specific payroll rates
  • Annual regulatory deadline calendar
  • Client employee handbook (custom)
🔁

Layer 3 — Escalation Logic

Certain situations are never handled by AI alone. The agent identifies them and hands off to a licensed professional automatically.

  • Termination disputes and severance calculations
  • Labour tribunal referrals
  • Disciplinary actions requiring HR authority
  • Cross-border multi-jurisdiction edge cases

05 — Hard Limits

What our AI employees will never do.

Compliance also means knowing the boundary. These restrictions are non-negotiable across every tier, every role, and every client.

Never practice law

AI agents can reference approved legal documents and flag compliance risks. They will never provide a legal opinion, draft a legally binding agreement without human review, or represent a business in a dispute.

Never act on people autonomously

Hiring, firing, disciplinary action, and leave approval are always human decisions. AI presents, humans decide. This is hard-coded — not a recommendation.

Never fabricate regulatory data

If a filing deadline, contribution rate, or legislative reference is not in the approved knowledge base, the agent says so and escalates. It does not guess.

Never cross client data boundaries

Employee records, payroll data, and business financials are isolated per client. No agent ever accesses or references data from another client deployment.
